EGR VALVE (DC MOTOR) CHECK
2020 / L200/L200 SPORTERO / EU - Workshop Manual
17 -ENGINE AND EMISSION CONTROL > EMISSION CONTROL <DIESEL> > EXHAUST GAS RECIRCULATION (EGR) SYSTEM<4N15> > EGR VALVE (DC MOTOR) CHECK
 
 
EGR Valve Operating Noise Check
noteCarry out this check after confirming that the diagnosis code is not set. If the diagnosis code is set, the check on the EGR valve (DC motor) would not possibly be carried out correctly.
1. Start the engine and warm up. Allow the engine coolant temperature to reach above 70°C.
2. Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position. Confirm that the operating noise of the EGR valve, such as the clicking noise, can be heard three times when the engine is stopped.
3. If the operating noise cannot be heard, check the driving circuit of the EGR valve (DC motor).
noteIf the operating noise cannot be heard although the circuit is normal, there can possibly be something wrong with the EGR valve.
 
 
 
EGR Valve Operational Check
1. Prepare the power supply (5 to 9 V) for the check.
2. Remove the EGR valve pipe A (Refer to ).
3. Disconnect the EGR valve connector.
4.
cautionAt this time, the power supply voltage is to be 5 V. For each voltage, current is to be applied within 5 seconds. Once the EGR valve is opened, the voltage is not to be increased any more. The supply voltage to the motor must be a maximum of 9 V.
Connect the terminal No. 4 of the EGR valve connector with the positive terminal of the power supply of the check (5 V). Connect the terminal No. 6 of the EGR valve connector with the negative terminal of the power supply of the check.
5. Apply the power supply of 5 V to the EGR valve. Confirm that the EGR valve is opened. If the EGR valve is not opened, increase the voltage of the power supply for the check by 1 V each time.
noteIf the EGR valve is not opened although the voltage applied to the motor is increased to 9 V, clean the EGR valve and carry out the operation check again. Despite this, replace the EGR valve if it does not operate.
6. Install the EGR valve pipe A.
 
 
 
EGR Valve Vacuum Actuator Check
1. Remove the EGR valve pipe B (Refer to ).
2. Connect the hand vacuum pump with the nipple of the vacuum actuator of the EGR valve.
3. Gradually applying the vacuum pressure, confirm through the hole produced by removing the EGR valve pipe B that the bypass valve shaft of the EGR valve is normally operated. When the vacuum pressure reaches 70 kPa, confirm that the vacuum pressure is maintained.
4. If the bypass valve shaft of the EGR valve is not operated, replace the EGR valve.
5. Install the EGR valve pipe B.
 
 
 
EGR Valve Cleaning
caution
  • Do not use the solvent or cleaner because it enters to the motor and causes the operational malfunction.
  • The cleaned portion must be the EGR valve only at the DC motor driven side from where all the circumference of the EGR valve can be seen. The EGR valve at the vacuum actuator driven side from where all the circumstance of the EGR valve cannot be seen results in the non-uniform cleaning. Do not clean the EGR valve at the vacuum actuator driven side because malfunctions can possibly happen such as the leak at the seat or foreign materials caught.
1. Remove the EGR valve (Refer to ).
2. Confirm that there is not the carbon stuck or accumulated on the EGR valve (at the DC motor driven side). If the carbon is stuck or accumulated on the EGR valve (at the DC motor driven side), use the wire brush and so on to clean the EGR valve.
3. Install the EGR valve.
