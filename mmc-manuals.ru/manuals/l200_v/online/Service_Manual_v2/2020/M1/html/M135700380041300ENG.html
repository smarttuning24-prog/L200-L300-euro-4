Inspection Procedure 16: HSA Works on a Flat Road.
 
 
caution
  • Whenever ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
  • When the hydraulic unit (integrated with ASTC-ECU) is replaced, always carry out the calibration of the master cylinder pressure sensor, steering wheel sensor and the G and yaw rate sensor (Refer to ).
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
  • A failure may have occurred in the calibration of the G & yaw rate sensor.
  • Some drivers mistake a brake dragging for HSA operation.
  • If the suspension is modified, the vehicle posture changes, and the ASTC-ECU judges that the vehicle is on a steep slope and may activate HSA.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • The G and yaw rate sensor improperly calibrated
  • Brake drag
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. HSA function check.
Check if the HSA works with 1 person on the driver's seat.
Does the HSA work on a flat road?
Go to Step 2.
This diagnosis is complete.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list.
Evaluate the data list below with 1 person on the driver's seat and the vehicle otherwise unloaded (unladen weight) on a smooth flat surface (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 19: Longitudinal G sensor (Raw value)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check the brake system for brake drag.
Check the brake system for brake drag.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the brake drag. Then go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. Retest the system.
Check if the HSA works with 1 person on the driver's seat.
Does the HSA work on a flat road?
Return to Step 2.
This diagnosis is complete.
