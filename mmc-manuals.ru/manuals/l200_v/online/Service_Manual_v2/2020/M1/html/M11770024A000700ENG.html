Inspection Procedure 1: The Communication Between M.U.T.-III and AS&G-ECU Not Possible
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The battery voltage is supplied to the diagnosis connector from the battery.
  • The diagnosis connector earth terminals are earthed to the vehicle body.
 
 
COMMENT ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the communication between M.U.T.-III and the AS&G-ECU is impossible, the CAN bus line, power supply circuit of the diagnosis connector, and/or earthing circuit could possibly be defective. The communication cannot be achieved either if the wrong vehicle type is selected on M.U.T.-III.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in the diagnosis connector circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed CAN communication.
  • Failed M.U.T.-III.
  • Failed AS&G-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the Vehicle Communication Interface (V.C.I.) operations
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
  • Connect the M.U.T.-III to the diagnosis connector.
  • Turn ON the power of the V.C.I.
OK: The indicator lamp of the V.C.I. illuminates in green.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check battery
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery, On-vehicle Service, Battery Test ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Replace the battery.
 
STEP 3. Perform voltage measurement at diagnosis connector
Voltage between terminal diagnosis connector power supply line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector(s), or check and repair the harness of open circuit, and short circuit in power supply line between diagnosis connector and battery.
 
STEP 4. Perform resistance measurement at diagnosis connector
Resistance between terminal diagnosis connector earthing line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
  • Repair the connector(s), or check of open circuit, and harness damage in earthing line between diagnosis connector and body earth.
 
STEP 5. Check of harness damage in power supply line between battery and diagnosis connector
Is the check result normal?
Check M.U.T.-III, refer to the M.U.T.-III User’s Manual.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 6. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnosis
Use M.U.T.-III to perform CAN bus line diagnosis.
Is the check result normal?
Check AS&G-ECU Power Supply, Ignition Switch-IG1 System (Refer to ).
Repair CAN bus line (Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting <CAN>, CAN Bus Diagnostics Table ).
