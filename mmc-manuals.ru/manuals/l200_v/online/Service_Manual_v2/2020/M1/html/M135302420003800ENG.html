Inspection Procedure 19: The HDC is Not Turned ON or OFF by Operating the HDC Switch. <Vehicles with HDC Function>
 
 
caution
  • If there is any problem in the CAN bus lines, an incorrect diagnosis code may be set. Prior to this diagnosis, diagnose the CAN bus lines (Refer to GROUP 54C - CAN Bus Diagnostics Table ).
  • Whenever ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
  • When the hydraulic unit (integrated with ASTC-ECU) is replaced, always carry out the calibration of the steering wheel sensor and the G and yaw rate sensor (Refer to ).
 
 
OPERATION
ON and OFF signals of the HDC switch are sent to ASTC-ECU from the gateway-ECU via CAN bus line.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
  • The HDC switch input circuit fails.
  • The HDC cannot be turned ON under any of the conditions: A warning notice is displayed on the multi-information display in the combination meter.
    1. Vehicle speed exceeds 20 km/h.
    2. Rear differential lock system: Lock
    3. The brake pads are hot.
    4. The ASTC-ECU detects a malfunction.
    5. The FCM is activated
  • When the HDC switch is pressed for 15 seconds or more, or the HDC switch is being pressed for long time in a certain condition such as something is put onto the HDC switch.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • When the ASTC-ECU stores the condition in which the HDC switch is being pressed for long time
  • Damaged wiring harness or connector
  • Malfunction of HDC switch
  • Malfunction of the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Restart the HDC
ASTC-ECU stores the condition, in which the HDC switch is being pressed for long time, when the HDC switch is being pressed for 15 seconds or more, or the HDC switch is being pressed for long time in a certain condition such as something is put onto the HDC switch. If you operate the switch in the condition above, HDC can not be turned ON/OFF, so perform the following procedures to clear the HDC switch condition, in which the HDC switch is being pressed for long time, stored in the ASTC-ECU.
(1) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Restart the engine.
(3) Is the HDC turned ON when you operate the HDC switch while all of the following conditions are satisfied?
  • Stop the vehicle.
  • Rear differential lock system: Unlock
Is the HDC restarted by operating the HDC switch?
This diagnosis is complete.
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnosis
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use the M.U.T.-III to diagnose the CAN bus lines.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the CAN bus lines (Refer to GROUP 54C - CAN Bus Diagnostics Table ). Then go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Retest the system
Is the HDC turned ON when you operate the HDC switch while all of the following conditions are satisfied?
  • Stop the vehicle.
  • Rear differential lock system: Unlock
Is the check result normal?
This diagnosis is complete.
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
Use M.U.T.-III to check the diagnosis code.
Is any diagnosis code set?
Perform the troubleshooting for the diagnosis code (Refer to Diagnosis Code Chart ). Then go to Step 8.
Go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. Check HDC switch itself
Check HDC switch itself (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the HDC switch (Refer to ). Then go to Step 8.
 
STEP 6. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to Data List Reference Table ).
  • Item No. 36: HDC switch
OK:
  • ON (When the HDC switch is pushed)
  • OFF (When the HDC switch is not pushed)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Perform the gateway-ECU input signal procedure {Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting <Gateway (for GW-CAN-C)>, Input Signal Procedures, Inspection Procedure 2: The HDC switch signal is not received }. Then go to Step 8.
 
STEP 7. Retest the system
Is the hill descent control turned on when you operate the hill descent control switch while all of the following conditions are satisfied?
  • Stop the vehicle.
  • Rear differential lock system: Unlock
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU) (Refer to ). Then go to Step 8 .
 
STEP 8. Retest the system
Is the hill descent control turned on when you operate the hill descent control switch while all of the following conditions are satisfied?
  • Stop the vehicle.
  • Rear differential lock system: Unlock
Is the check result normal?
This diagnosis is complete.
Return to Step 1.
