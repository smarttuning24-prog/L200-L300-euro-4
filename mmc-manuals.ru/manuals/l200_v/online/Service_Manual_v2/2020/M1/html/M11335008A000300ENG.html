Code No. P0118: Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor Circuit High Input
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
Refer to Code No. P0117: Engine Coolant Temperature Sensor Circuit Low Input .
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15> converts the engine coolant temperature into a voltage signal, and inputs the voltage to the engine-ECU.
  • In response to the signal, the engine-ECU controls the fuel infection amount and the fast idle speed when the engine is cold state.
  • The engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15> is a kind of resistor, which has characteristics to reduce its resistance as the engine coolant temperature rises. Therefore, the sensor output voltage varies with the engine coolant temperature, and becomes lower as the engine coolant temperature rises.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V
  • 2 seconds later after the ignition switch has been in "ON" position or the engine has started up.
Judgement Criterion
  • The engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15> output voltage is 4.95 V or more.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The engine coolant temperature is controlled to be 80°C (Start-up of -25°C).
  • The throttle valve is opened (Electrical discontinuity).
  • The EGR control is stopped (Fully closed).
  • The DPF regeneration is prohibited.
  • The AS&G control is prohibited.
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15>
  • Open circuit or harness damage in engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15> circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 7: Engine coolant temperature sensor
OK:
  • Engine cold state: At ambient temperature (atmospheric temperature) or equivalent.
  • Engine hot state: At 80 to 120°C
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Perform resistance measurement at engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor connector <4N15>
  • Engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor connector <4N15> check.
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at sensor side.
  • Resistance between terminals.
OK:
  • Engine coolant temperature at -20°C: 14 to 17 kΩ
  • (Engine coolant temperature at 0°C: 5.1 to 6.5 kΩ)
  • Engine coolant temperature at 20°C: 2.1 to 2.7 kΩ
  • (Engine coolant temperature at 40°C: 0.9 to 1.3 kΩ)
  • (Engine coolant temperature at 60°C: 0.48 to 0.68 kΩ)
  • (Engine coolant temperature at 80°C: 0.26 to 0.36 kΩ)
note( ): Reference value.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor <4N15>.
 
STEP 3. Perform voltage measurement at engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor connector <4N15>
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU WTS line and body earth.
OK: 4.5 to 4.9 V
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal WTS <4N14> or between engine coolant temperature sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal WTS <4N15>
Check output line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 7: Engine coolant temperature sensor
OK:
  • Engine cold state: At ambient temperature (atmospheric temperature) or equivalent.
  • Engine hot state: At 80 to 120°C
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
 
STEP 6. Perform resistance measurement at engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector <4N14> or engine coolant temperature sensor connector <4N15>
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between terminal engine-ECU WTSE line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. Check harness between engine coolant temperature sensor No. 1 connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal WTSE <4N14> or between engine coolant temperature sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal WTSE <4N15>
Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
