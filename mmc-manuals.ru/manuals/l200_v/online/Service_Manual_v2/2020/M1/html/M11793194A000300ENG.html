Inspection Procedure 4: Diagnose Shorts Between CAN_H and CAN_L Lines (ENG-LOCAL CAN).
 
 
cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT CONDITIONS
When all of the following conditions are met, the M.U.T.-III determines that a short circuit is present between CAN_L and CAN_H lines.
  • The M.U.T.-III cannot receive the periodic transmission data from DCU.
  • The voltage between ENG-LOCAL CAN (CAN_L and CAN_H lines) is 1.0 V or more, 4.0 V or less.
  • The resistance at ENG-LOCAL CAN (CAN_L and CAN_H lines) is 2 Ω or less.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the wiring harness
  • Malfunction of the connector
  • Malfunction in the ECUs or sensors which are interconnected via ENG-LOCAL CAN (internal error) (DCU, Engine-ECU, No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, No. 2 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, exhaust muffler PM sensor)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Connector check: joint connector (CAN4)
cautionThe strand end of the twist wire should be within 10 cm from the connector (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
When checking the joint connector (CAN4), ensure that its wiring harness side and its short pins are not damaged.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair a defective connector or replace the joint connector (CAN4).
 
STEP 2. Resistance measurement at joint connector (CAN4).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(3) Disconnect joint connector (CAN4), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(4) Resistance between joint connector (CAN4) terminal No. 11 and No. 22.
OK: 120 ± 20 Ω
Is the check result normal?
YES <Within 120 ± 20 Ω> : Go to Step 4.
NO <Not within 120 ± 20 Ω> : Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Resistance measurement at engine-ECU connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • Strictly observe the specified wiring harness repair procedure (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Remove the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU <4N14>), and measure at the equipment side.
(2) Resistance between engine-ECU connector terminal LCAH and LCAL.
OK: 120 ± 20 Ω
Is the check result normal?
YES <Within 120 ± 20 Ω> : Repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN4) and the engine-ECU connector.
NO <Not within 120 ± 20 Ω> : Check the engine-ECU connector, and repair if necessary. If the engine-ECU connector is in good condition, replace the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU <4N14>).

Is the check result normal?

NO <Less than 1 kΩ > : Go to Step 5.
