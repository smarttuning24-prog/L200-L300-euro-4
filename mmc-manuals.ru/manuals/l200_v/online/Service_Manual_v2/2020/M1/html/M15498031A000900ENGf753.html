Inspection Procedure 2: The LDW does not enter the warning monitor mode under some circumstances.
 
 
caution
  • The system may not also work correctly under certain external factors (such as weather, road conditions, or ambient environments). Prior to the diagnosis, grasp the details if necessary.
  • Before replacing the ECU, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the LDW does not enter the warning monitor mode, any of the followings are suspected: bad weather (heavy rain, snow, icing), unfavourable road condition, ambient environment, blocked FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU sensor or its surroundings (scratches, contamination, icing, fog on the windshield or the sensor), or a faulty FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU.
noteThe system does not proceed to the warning monitor mode when any of the followings is satisfied:
  • Narrow road (width: less than 2.5 mm)
  • The road where white and yellow lines intersect
  • The road where the distances among 2 white and 1 yellow lines are narrow
  • The road where the distances among 1 white and 2 yellow lines are narrow
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Faults on the windshield glass (such as scratch, cracks, contamination or drain drops on the glass surface)
  • FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU improperly installed
  • Faulty FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU (sensor: lane improperly detected)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the sensor mounted surface on the windshield.
Visually check the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU mounted surface on the windshield for scratch, cracks, contamination or drain drops.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the Windshield (Refer to GROUP 42A - Windshield ).
 
STEP 2. Check whether the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU is installed properly.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Install the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU properly.
 
STEP 3. M.U.T.-III data list
Check the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU data list (Refer to ).
  • The vehicle is being driven on the road where lane division lines are clear with the LDW activated.

STEP 4. Retest the system.
Check that the LDW operates normally.
Is the check result normal?
This symptom is caused by a bad weather or road conditions. It is not a malfunction.
