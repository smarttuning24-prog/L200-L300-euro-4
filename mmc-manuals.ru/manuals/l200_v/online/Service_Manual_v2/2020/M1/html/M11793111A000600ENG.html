Code No. P203D: Urea Tank Level Sensor Circuit High
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • A power is applied to the urea tank level sensor/urea tank temperature sensor power supply terminal of urea pump module connector from the DCU connector terminal TLSS and earthed to the DCU connector terminal TLSE from the urea tank level sensor/urea tank temperature sensor earth terminal of urea pump module connector.
  • The PWM signal of sensor is inputted to the DCU connector terminal TLS from the urea tank level sensor/urea tank temperature sensor output terminal of urea pump module connector.
 
 
FUNCTION
The DCU sets diagnosis code No. P203D when a signal indicating urea tank level sensor abnormality (maximum abnormality) is received.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position.
  • At least 1 second has passed since the DCU was activated.
Judgement Criterion
  • A short to power supply signal of the urea tank level sensor is received from the urea tank level sensor for 2 seconds or more.
Check Conditions
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position.
  • DCU supply voltage is 10 V or more.
  • At least 1 second has passed since the DCU was activated.
Judgement Criterion
  • The self-diagnosis result pulse signal indicating maximum abnormality is received from the urea tank level sensor 3 times or more.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Open/short circuit in urea tank level sensor circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed urea tank assembly (integrated with urea tank level sensor)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Perform voltage measurement at urea pump module connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal DCU TLSS line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 2. Check harness between DUC connector terminal TLSE and urea pump module connector terminal
Check earth line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between DCU connector terminal TLS and urea pump module connector terminal
Check sensor line for open circuit, short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between DCU connector terminal TLSS and urea pump module connector terminal
Check power supply line for open circuit, short to earth and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Replace the urea tank assembly (integrated with urea tank level sensor) (Refer to ), and then check whether the urea tank level is displayed correctly.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
