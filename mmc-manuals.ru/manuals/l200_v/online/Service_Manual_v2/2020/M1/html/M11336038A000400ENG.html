Inspection Procedure 16: Engine-ECU Power Supply System <4N15>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The battery voltage is applied to the engine control relay connector terminal.
  • The engine-ECU connector terminal C/R makes the power transistor in the unit be in ON position and makes currents go on the engine control relay coil, and that makes the relay be in ON position.
  • When the engine control relay is in ON position, the battery voltage is supplied to the engine-ECU connector terminal BAT1 and terminal BAT2, the sensor and the actuator from the engine control relay connector terminal.
  • The engine-ECU connector terminal GNDB, terminal GNDE and terminal SGND is earthing to the vehicle body.
 
 
FUNCTION
When the ignition switch "ON" signal is input into the engine-ECU, the engine-ECU turns ON the engine control relay. This causes battery voltage to be supplied to the engine-ECU, sensor and actuator.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed battery
  • Failed engine control relay
  • Failed ignition switch <Vehicles without KOS>
  • Failed KOS&OSS-ECU <Vehicles with KOS>
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in engine control relay circuit, or loose connector contact.
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in ignition switch (IG1) circuit, or loose connector contact.
  • Open circuit or harness damage in engine-ECU earth circuit, or loose connector contact.
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check battery
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery, On-vehicle Service, Battery Test ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the battery.
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at engine control relay connector
  • Remove relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Voltage between terminal No. 2 (power supply line) and body earth
  • Voltage between terminal No. 3 (power supply line) and body earth
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair harness between battery and engine control relay connector terminal.
    • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
 
STEP 3. Perform voltage measurement at engine-ECU connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Voltage between terminal C/R and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between engine control relay connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal C/R
Check output line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. Check engine control relay itself
  • Engine control relay connector check.
  • Engine control relay continuity check (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the engine control relay.
 
STEP 6. Perform voltage measurement at engine-ECU connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Short-circuit engine-ECU connector terminal C/R to the body earth.
  • Voltage between terminal BAT1, BAT2 and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. Check harness between engine control relay connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal BAT1, BAT2
Check output line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 8. Check engine control relay itself
  • Engine control relay connector check.
  • Engine control relay continuity check (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the engine control relay.
 
STEP 9. Perform voltage measurement at engine-ECU connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal IGN and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
NO <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 10.
NO <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 11.
 
STEP 10. Check ignition switch itself <Vehicles without KOS>
  • Ignition switch connector check.
  • Check ignition switch itself (Refer to GROUP 54A - Ignition Switch, Ignition Switch, Ignition Switch Check ).
Is the check result normal?
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair the harness between ignition switch connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal IGN.
    • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the ignition switch.
 
STEP 11. Check IG1 relay itself <Vehicles with KOS>
  • IG1 relay connector check.
  • Check IG1 relay itself (Refer to GROUP 42B - On-vehicle Service, Relay Inspection ).
Is the check result normal?
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair the harness between IG1 relay connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal IGN.
    • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the IG1 relay.
 
STEP 12. Check harness between engine-ECU connector terminal GNDB, GNDE, SGND and body earth
Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 13. Check the trouble symptoms
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the engine-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
 
STEP 14. Check the trouble symptoms
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Go to Step 1.
The check is end.
