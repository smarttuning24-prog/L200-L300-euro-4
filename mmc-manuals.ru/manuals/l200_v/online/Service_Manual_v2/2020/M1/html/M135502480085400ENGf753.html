Inspection Procedure 13: ASTC System Operates too Frequently.
 
 
caution
  • Whenever ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
  • When the hydraulic unit (integrated with ASTC-ECU) is replaced, always carry out the calibration of the master cylinder pressure sensor, steering wheel sensor and the G and yaw rate sensor (Refer to ).
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
  • Although the cause of the trouble cannot be clearly resolved since it depends on the running status and road surface condition, the malfunction of the hydraulic circuit may occur if any diagnosis code is not detected.
  • If wheels and tyres other than the ones with genuine specified size are mounted, the ASTC system may be activated prematurely.
  • If a non-genuine braking device or non-genuine impact reduction device is mounted, the ASTC system may be activated prematurely.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Mounting of wheels and tyres other than with genuine specified size
  • Tyre pressure abnormality
  • Tyre wear and deterioration
  • Mounting of non-genuine braking device or impact reduction device
  • Wheel alignment abnormality
  • Malfunction of the brake related parts
  • Malfunction of the wheel speed sensor
  • Malfunction of the steering wheel sensor
  • Malfunction of the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU)
  • Malfunction of hydraulic circuit
  • External radio wave noise interference
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the wheels and the tyres.
Carry out the following check.
  • Size check of wheels and tyres
  • Tyre wear and deterioration statuses
  • Check the tyre pressure.
    noteFor the tyre pressure, refer to the tyre pressure label attached to the lower section of driver's side door striker.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Correct the wheels and tyres in good condition. Then go to Step 9.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the diagnosis code for the ASTC system.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Carry out the diagnosis for the diagnosis code (Refer to ). Then go to Step 9.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check of brake system related components other than hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU).
At the same time with the following checks, also check that no parts other than the genuine parts are mounted.
  • Brake pad check (Refer to GROUP 35A - On-vehicle Service, Brake Pad Check ).
  • Brake disc runout check (Refer to GROUP 35A - On-vehicle Service, Brake Disc Check ).
  • Brake drag force check (Refer to GROUP 35A - Front Disc Brake Assembly <1-Piston> <Front (1-Piston)> or Front Disc Brake Assembly <2-Piston> <Front (2-Piston)>, and GROUP 35A - Rear Drum Brake Assembly <rear>).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the part(s) having damage or other problems. Then go to Step 9 .
 
STEP 4. Wheel alignment check.
At the same time with the following checks, also check that no parts other than the genuine parts are mounted.
  • Front wheel alignment check (Refer to GROUP 33 - On-vehicle Service, Front Wheel Alignment Check and Adjustment ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the part(s) having damage or other problems. Then go to Step 9 .
 
STEP 5. Wheel speed sensor check.
Check that no non-genuine electronic device or no wiring harness of the device other than genuine electronic device is mounted near the wheel speed sensor (at wheel speed detection section and wiring harness section) (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Remove the non-genuine electronic device or the wiring harness of non-genuine electronic device. Then go to Step 9.
 
STEP 6. M.U.T.-III data list.
Check the following data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 04: Yaw rate sensor
  • Item No. 12: FL wheel speed sensor
  • Item No. 13: FR wheel speed sensor
  • Item No. 14: RL wheel speed sensor
  • Item No. 15: RR wheel speed sensor
  • Item No. 20: Lateral G sensor (Raw value)
  • Item No. 21: Steering angle (Raw value)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
NO (Abnormality with items No. 12 to 15) : Check the installation status of corresponding wheel speed sensor (Refer to ).
NO (Abnormality with item No. 04 or 20) : Go to Step 7.
NO (Abnormality with item No. 21) : Check the installation status of steering wheel sensor (Refer to ).
 
STEP 7. Hydraulic unit check.
Carry out the following actuator tests, and check if they work normally (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 01: FL wheel ABS drive
  • Item No. 02: FR wheel ABS drive
  • Item No. 03: RL wheel ABS drive
  • Item No. 04: RR wheel ABS drive
  • Item No. 05: FL wheel TCL drive
  • Item No. 06: FR wheel TCL drive
  • Item No. 07: RL wheel TCL drive
  • Item No. 08: RR wheel TCL drive
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Replace the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU) (Refer to ). Then go to Step 9.
 
STEP 8. Retest the system.
Does ASTC system operation normally?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU) (Refer to ). Then go to Step 9.
 
STEP 9. Retest the system.
Does ASTC system operation normally?
This diagnosis is complete.
Return to Step 1.
