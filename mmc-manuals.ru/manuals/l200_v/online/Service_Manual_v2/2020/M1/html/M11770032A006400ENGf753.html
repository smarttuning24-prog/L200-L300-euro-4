Inspection Procedure 2: Starting Impossible (Starter Not Operative) <M/T>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The ignition switch-"ST" signal is inputted into the AS&G-ECU connector terminal ST from the ignition switch connector terminal ST <vehicles without KOS> or KOS&OSS-ECU connector terminal STO <vehicles with KOS>.
  • The battery voltage is supplied to the starter cut relay terminal from the battery.
  • The AS&G-ECU connector terminal STCR turns ON the power transistor in the unit, and then makes the current flow into the starter cut relay terminal to turn ON the relay.
  • When the starter cut relay is turned ON, the battery voltage is supplied to the starter relay terminal from the starter cut relay terminal.
  • The AS&G-ECU connector terminal STRL turns ON the power transistor in the unit, and then make the current flow into the starter relay terminal to turn ON the relay.
  • When the starter cut relay and starter relay are turned ON, the battery voltage is supplied to the starter connector terminal from the starter relay terminal.
  • The starter cut relay terminal and the starter relay terminal are earthed to the vehicle body.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
The failure could possibly be caused by the malfunction of the starter or starter system circuits. If the engine-ECU or the KOS&OSS-ECU <vehicles with KOS> sets the diagnosis code, the engine start should be restricted.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed battery
  • Failed clutch interlock switch
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in clutch interlock switch circuit, or loose connector contact
  • Failed ignition switch.
  • Failed starter cut relay
  • Failed starter relay
  • Failed starter assembly.
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in ignition switch-"ST" circuit, or loose connector contact
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in starter cut relay circuit, or loose connector contact
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in starter relay circuit, or loose connector contact
  • Failed diesel fuel system.
  • Failed KOS. <vehicles with KOS>
  • Failed AS&G-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check battery
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery, On-vehicle Service, Battery Test ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the battery.
 
STEP 2. Check for diagnosis code of other system
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check if the diagnosis code is set in the diesel fuel system.
Is any diagnosis code set in the diesel fuel system?
Perform the troubleshooting of the diesel fuel system (Refer to GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Inspection Chart for Diagnosis Code ).
NO <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 4.
NO <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check for diagnosis code of other system <Vehicles with KOS>
Use M.U.T.-III to check if the diagnosis code is set in the KOS&OSS-ECU.
Is any diagnosis code set in the KOS?
Perform the troubleshooting of the KOS&OSS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 42B - Troubleshooting, Diagnosis Code Chart ).
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 7: Clutch SW Bottom
OK:
  • ON (Clutch pedal: Fully depressed)
  • OFF (Clutch pedal: Released)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Perform the troubleshooting of diagnosis code No. P0833: Clutch interlock switch circuit (Refer to ).
 
STEP 5. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 1: Starter SW
  • Clutch pedal: Fully depressed
OK:
  • ON (Ignition switch: "ST" position)
  • OFF (Ignition switch: "ON" position)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Perform voltage measurement at AS&G-ECU connector by back probing
  • Do not disconnect connector.
  • Clutch pedal: Fully depressed
  • Ignition switch: "ST" position
  • Voltage between terminal AS&G-ECU ST line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
NO <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 7.
NO <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 8.
 
STEP 7. Check ignition switch itself <Vehicles without KOS>
Check ignition switch itself (Refer to GROUP 54A - Ignition Switch, Inspection, Ignition Switch Check ).
Is the check result normal?
  • Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit, and harness damage in ST line between ignition switch connector and AS&G-ECU connector.
Replace the ignition switch.
 
STEP 8. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in STO line between KOS&OSS-ECU connector and AS&G-ECU connector <Vehicles with KOS>
Is the check result normal?
Perform the troubleshooting of the KOS&OSS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 42B - Troubleshooting, Symptom Chart ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Check the trouble symptom
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the AS&G-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
 
STEP 10. Check the starter cut relay itself and starter relay itself
Check the starter cut relay itself and starter relay itself (Refer to GROUP 16 - Starting System, On-vehicle Service, Starter Relay Continuity Check ).
Are the check results normal?
Go to Step 11.
Replace the starter cut relay and/or starter relay.
 
STEP 11. Perform resistance measurement at starter cut relay connector
  • Remove the starter cut relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Resistance between terminal starter cut relay earthing line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
  • Repair the connector(s), or check of open circuit, and harness damage in earthing line between starter cut relay connector and body earth.
 
STEP 12. Perform resistance measurement at starter relay connector
  • Remove the starter relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Resistance between terminal starter relay earthing line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
  • Repair the connector(s), or check of open circuit, and harness damage in earthing line between starter relay connector and body earth.
 
STEP 13. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in power supply line between battery and starter cut relay connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 14. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in STCR line between AS&G-ECU connector and starter cut relay connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 15. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in output line between starter cut relay connector and starter relay connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 16. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in STRL line between AS&G-ECU connector and starter relay connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 17.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 17. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in NTSW line between starter relay connector and engine-ECU connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 18.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 18. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in output line between starter relay connector and starter connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 19. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in power supply line between battery and starter connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 20.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 20. Check starter assembly operation
Check starter assembly operation (Refer to GROUP 16 - Starting System, Starter Motor Assembly, Inspection ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Replace the starter assembly.
