FORCIBLE DPF REGENERATION <4N14>
2020 / L200/L200 SPORTERO / EU - Workshop Manual
17 -ENGINE AND EMISSION CONTROL > EMISSION CONTROL <DIESEL> > DIESEL PARTICULATE FILTER (DPF) SYSTEM > FORCIBLE DPF REGENERATION<4N14>
warning
  • When the forcible DPF regeneration is carried out, confirm that no people or no combustible material is within its vicinity. If people or combustible material is around the muffler, serious injury or damage such as heat injury or fires may occur because high temperature exhaust gases are put out.
  • Perform the forcible DPF regeneration in a well-ventilated and flat place when the vehicle is stopped (If perform the forcible DPF regeneration indoors, surely carry out enforced ventilation).
  • If the engine warning lamp is lit, the DPF system might be abnormal. Carry out the inspection chart for diagnosis code, and then perform the forcible DPF regeneration (Refer to , GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Inspection Chart For Diagnosis codes).
note
  • If white smoke or particular smell appear during the forcible DPF regeneration, this does not indicate an abnormality.
  • When the engine warning lamp illuminates with the DPF warning mark shown in the multi information display, the forcible regeneration of the DPF must be carried out.
  • If the forcible DPF regeneration is performed while the DPF warning mark is displayed due to the accumulation of PM, the DPF warning mark disappears when the forcible DPF regeneration is completed normally.
1. Set the vehicle to the pre-inspection condition.
2. Perform ammonia purge (Refer to ). After ammonia purge is complete, leave the vehicle with the ignition switch OFF until diesel fuel system data list Item No. 118: No. 2 exhaust gas temperature sensor falls below 115°C (Refer to GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Data List Reference Table).
note
  • There is no problem with not performing ammonia purge. However, a strong ammonia odour may be generated during the forcible DPF regeneration if ammonia purge is not performed.
  • Although it depends on the surrounding environment, it takes approximately 38 minutes for the No. 2 exhaust gas temperature sensor to fall below 115°C after ammonia purge is complete.
cautionConnection and disconnection of M.U.T.-III should be always performed with the ignition switch turned to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
3. After the ignition switch is in "LOCK" (OFF) position, connect the M.U.T.-III to the diagnosis connector.
4. Start the engine and let it run at idle.
5. Select "MPI/GDI/DIESEL" from System Select Screen of the M.U.T.-III.
6. From function menu on M.U.T.-III, select "Special Function".
7. From "Special Function", select "DPF regeneration".
cautionIf the forcible regeneration of the DPF is carried out while the accumulated amount of the PM in the DPF exceeds the limit, the forcible regeneration might not be correctly completed. This could be caused by the detection in the DPF system malfunction. Carry out the check procedure for the diagnosis code and solve the malfunction (Refer to , GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Inspection Chart For Diagnosis Codes).
8. Perform Item No. 34: DPF Regeneration.
noteCarrying out the forcible DPF regeneration increases the engine speed 1,500 to 2,000 r/min (the temperature increases within the DPF and is maintained at approximately 600°C).
9. When the DPF regeneration is started, disconnect the special tool V.C.I.-Lite USB cable (MB992747/MB992748) or M.U.T.-III USB cable (MB991827) from the personal computer, and then connect it again.
noteWhen the V.C.I.-Lite USB cable or M.U.T.-III USB cable is disconnected, the System Select Screen appears.
10. Select "DCU" from System Select Screen.
11. From DCU Screen, select "Special Function."
12. From Special Function Screen, select "DPF regeneration."
13. Carry out Item No.1 Start fixed-quantity Urea injection from DPF regeneration Screen of the M.U.T.-III.
noteDo not disconnect the M.U.T.-III from the diagnosis connector during the forcible DPF regeneration.
14. When the engine speed returns to the normal idle operation, check whether the DPF warning mark is displayed.
note
  • When performed for about 25 minutes, the forcible DPF regeneration automatically stops. Depressing the accelerator pedal during the regeneration, however, can cancel the forcible DPF regeneration.
  • Only when the DPF warning mark is not displayed, carry out Step 15, and then go to Step 21.
15. Let the engine idle until the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out. After the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out, turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
noteWhen the engine is run at idle for 15 minutes, the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out.
16. When the DPF warning mark does not go out, carry out the following procedures again.
(1) Leave the vehicle with the ignition switch OFF until diesel fuel system data list Item No. 118: No. 2 exhaust gas temperature sensor falls below 115°C (Refer to GROUP 13A - Troubleshooting, Data List Reference Table).
(2) Carry out step 8 to step 14.
17. When the DPF warning mark goes out, let the engine idle until the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out. After the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out, turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
noteWhen the engine is run at idle for 15 minutes, the diesel preheat indicator lamp goes out.
18. Check diesel fuel system data list Item No. 328: DPF regeneration request (Refer to GROUP 13C - Troubleshooting, Data List Reference Table).
noteOnly if the check result is ON (during DPF regeneration request), ask the customer to perform the DPF regeneration by driving when returning the vehicle. If it is difficult for the customer to perform the DPF regeneration, perform the forcible DPF regeneration again from Step 16.
19. Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position and then remove the M.U.T.-III.
