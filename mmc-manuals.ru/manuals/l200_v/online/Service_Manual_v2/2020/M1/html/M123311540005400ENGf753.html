DISASSEMBLY
 
 
1. OIL PAN SUB-ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
1. Remove the 10 bolts from the oil pan sub-assembly.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 25 mm

2.
caution
  • Be careful not to damage the fitting surfaces of the transmission case and the oil pan sub-assembly.
  • Be careful not to deform the oil pan sub-assembly.
Using a plastic hammer, tap the oil pan sub-assembly to remove it.
3. Remove the oil pan gasket.
 

 
 
2. OIL CLEANER MAGNET REMOVAL
Remove the 4 oil cleaner magnets from the oil pan sub-assembly.
note
  • Examine the chips and particles in the oil pan sub-assembly and on the oil cleaner magnet to determine what type of wear has occurred in the transmission.
    Steel (magnetic) = bearing, gear and plate wear.
    Brass (non-magnetic) = bushing wear.
  • Keep a record of the amount of fluid that is drained.
 

 
 
3. WIRING HARNESS REMOVAL
1. Remove the 2 bolts and the 2 temperature sensor clamps, and pull out the 2 temperature sensors from the valve body assembly. <Vehicles with AS&G system>
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
B: M6 × 1.0 × 36 mm
2. Disconnect the 8 solenoid connectors from the solenoids. <Vehicles with AS&G system>
3. Remove the 2 bolts and the 2 temperature sensor clamps, and pull out the 2 temperature sensors from the valve body assembly. <Vehicles without AS&G system>
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
B: M6 × 1.0 × 36 mm
4. Disconnect the 7 solenoid connectors from the solenoids. <Vehicles without AS&G system>

5.
caution
  • Be careful not to damage the wiring harness.
  • Be careful not to pull the wiring harness forcibly.
Remove the bolt from the transmission case.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
6. Remove the O-ring from the wiring harness connector.
7. Remove the 2 O-rings from the A/T fluid temperature sensor.
 

 
 
4. OIL STRAINER ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
1. Remove the 3 bolts to remove the oil strainer assembly from the valve body assembly.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 16 mm
2. Remove the O-ring from the oil strainer assembly.
 

 
 
5. MANUAL DETENT SPRING REMOVAL
Remove the bolt to remove the manual detent spring and the manual detent spring cover from the valve body assembly.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
 

 
 
6. VALVE BODY ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
cautionBe careful not to drop the valve body assembly.
Remove the 12 bolts and the valve body assembly from the transmission case.
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 25 mm
B: M6 × 1.0 × 36 mm
C: M6 × 1.0 × 50 mm
 

 
 
7. TRANSMISSION CASE GASKET REMOVAL
Remove the 2 transmission case gaskets from the transmission case.
 

 
 
8. BRAKE DRUM GASKET REMOVAL
Remove the 2 brake drum gaskets from the transmission case.
 

 
 
9. PARKING LOCK PAWL BRACKET REMOVAL
1. Remove the 3 bolts from the transmission case.
Bolt size:
M8 × 1.25 × 18.5 mm
2. Remove the parking lock pawl bracket from the transmission case.
noteWhen removing the parking lock pawl bracket, move the parking lock rod upward while pushing the parking lock pawl as shown in the illustration.
 

 
 
10. PARKING LOCK ROD REMOVAL
Disconnect the parking lock rod from the manual valve lever.
 

 
 
11. PARKING LOCK PAWL SHAFT REMOVAL
1.
cautionBe careful not to apply too much force to the torsion spring.
Remove the torsion spring from the parking lock pawl shaft.
2. Remove the parking lock pawl shaft and parking lock pawl from the transmission case.
3. Remove the E-ring from the parking lock pawl shaft.
 

 
 
12. MANUAL VALVE LEVER REMOVAL
1.
cautionBe careful not to damage the manual valve lever shaft.
Using a screwdriver and hammer, cut off the spacer and remove it from the manual valve lever shaft.
2. Using a pin punch (3 mm) and hammer, tap out the spring pin.
noteSlowly tap out the spring pin so that it does not fall into the transmission case.
3. Pull the manual valve lever shaft out through the transmission case, and remove the manual valve lever.
 

 
 
13. MANUAL VALVE LEVER SHAFT OIL SEAL REMOVAL
caution
  • Be careful not to damage the transmission case.
  • Tape the screwdriver tip before use.
  • If using a screwdriver, use a wooden block or equivalent to avoid damaging the fitting surface of the transmission case.
Using a screwdriver, pry out the 2 manual valve lever shaft oil seals from the transmission case.
 
 
 
 
REASSEMBLY
 
 
1. MANUAL VALVE LEVER SHAFT OIL SEAL INSTALLATION
1. Apply yellow vaseline to the lip of 2 new manual valve lever shaft oil seals.
2.
caution
  • Be careful not to damage the manual valve lever shaft oil seal.
  • Be careful not to damage the transmission case.
  • Be careful not to damage the manual valve lever shaft.
  • Press in the oil seal to the specified depth, being careful not to tilt it.
Using special tool drag link bushing remover and installer B (MB990842) and a hammer, install the 2 manual valve lever shaft oil seals to the transmission case.
Standard value: 0 ± 0.5 mm
(From the end of the transmission case)
 

 
 
2. MANUAL VALVE LEVER INSTALLATION
1. Install a new spacer to the manual valve lever.
2. Install the manual valve lever shaft to the transmission case through the manual valve lever.
3. Using a pin punch (3 mm) and hammer, tap in a new spring pin.
4. Align the manual valve lever indentation with the spacer hole, and stake them together with a punch.
5. Make sure that the shaft rotates smoothly.
 

 
 
3. PARKING LOCK PAWL SHAFT INSTALLATION
1. Install the E-ring to the parking lock pawl shaft.
2. Install the parking lock pawl and the parking lock pawl shaft to the transmission case.
3.
cautionBe careful not to apply too much force to the torsion spring.
Install the torsion spring to the parking lock pawl shaft.
 

 
 
4. PARKING LOCK ROD INSTALLATION
Connect the parking lock rod to the manual valve lever.
 

 
 
5. PARKING LOCK PAWL BRACKET INSTALLATION
1. Install the parking lock pawl bracket with the 3 bolts.
Bolt size:
M8 × 1.25 × 18.5 mm
noteWhen installing the parking lock pawl bracket, move the parking lock rod upward while pushing the parking lock pawl as shown in the illustration.
2. Tighten the 3 bolts to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 18 ± 2 N·m
 

 
 
6. BRAKE DRUM GASKET INSTALLATION
1. Apply ATF to 2 new brake drum gaskets.
2. Install the 2 brake drum gaskets to the transmission case.
 

 
 
7. TRANSMISSION CASE GASKET INSTALLATION
1. Apply ATF to 2 new transmission case gaskets.
2. Install the 2 transmission case gaskets to the transmission case.
noteWhen installing the transmission case gasket, make sure that the notched side of the transmission case gasket faces the transmission case side.
 

 
 
8. VALVE BODY ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
1. Insert the manual valve lever into the groove on the end of the manual valve.
2. Install the valve body assembly with 12 new bolts to the transmission case.
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 25 mm
B: M6 × 1.0 × 36 mm
C: M6 × 1.0 × 50 mm
3. Tighten the 12 bolts to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 11 ± 1 N·m
 

 
 
9. MANUAL DETENT SPRING INSTALLATION
cautionMake sure to align the centre of the detent spring roller with the centre of the manual valve lever.
Install the detent spring and detent spring cover to the transmission case by tightening it to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 10 ± 2 N·m
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
 

 
 
10. OIL STRAINER ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
1. Apply ATF to a new O-ring, and install it to the oil strainer assembly.
O-ring size:
 
Inner diameter (mm)Thickness (mm)
27.732.62
2. Install the oil strainer assembly to the valve body assembly with the 3 bolts.
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 16 mm
3. Tighten the 3 bolts to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 10 ± 2 N·m
 

 
 
11. WIRING HARNESS INSTALLATION
1. Apply ATF to a new O-ring, and install it to the wiring harness.
O-ring size:
 
Inner diameter (mm)Thickness (mm)
36.82.4
2. Apply ATF to 2 new O-rings, and install it to the A/T fluid temperature sensor.
O-ring size:
 
Inner diameter (mm)Thickness (mm)
6.11.9
3. Install the wiring harness to the transmission case with the bolt.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
4. Tighten the bolt to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 5.4 ± 1.5 N·m
5. Connect the 8 solenoid connectors to the solenoids. <Vehicles with AS&G system>
6. Connect the 7 solenoid connectors to the solenoids. <Vehicles without AS&G system>
7. Install the 2 oil temperature sensors and the 2 temperature sensor clamps to the valve body assembly with the 2 bolts.
Bolt size:
A: M6 × 1.0 × 14 mm
B: M6 × 1.0 × 36 mm
8. Tighten the 2 bolts to the specified torque.
Tightening torque:
A: 10 ± 2 N·m
B: 11 ± 1 N·m
 

 
 
12. OIL CLEANER MAGNET INSTALLATION
Install the 4 oil cleaner magnets to the oil pan sub-assembly.
note
  • Clean the oil pan sub-assembly.
  • Clean the oil cleaner magnet before install it.
 

 
 
13. OIL PAN SUB-ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
1. Clean oil off the oil pan gasket fitting surface of the transmission case and the 10 bolt holes.
noteCompletely remove oil with white gasoline or similar.
2.
caution
  • Be careful not to damage the fitting surfaces of the transmission case and the oil pan sub-assembly.
  • Be careful not to deform the oil pan sub-assembly.
Install a new oil pan gasket and the transmission oil pan sub-assembly to the transmission case.
3.
caution
  • Make sure that there is no oil or foreign matter on the oil pan gasket seal surface and the oil pan sub-assembly contact surface.
  • Install the oil pan gasket so that there is no slack in the oil pan gasket, and that the seal surface's entire circumference is level.
  • Make sure that the oil pan gasket drop prevention protrusions are set on the oil pan sub-assembly.
  • When tightening the oil pan sub-assembly, make sure that the oil pan gasket is not pinched between the oil pan gasket tightening area's sleeve and the transmission case seal surface.
Install the 10 bolts to the transmission case.
Bolt size:
M6 × 1.0 × 25 mm
4. Tighten the 10 bolts to the specified torque.
noteBe sure to tighten the bolts evenly and gradually, in a diagonally opposing sequence.
Tightening torque: 7.4 ± 1.4 N·m
