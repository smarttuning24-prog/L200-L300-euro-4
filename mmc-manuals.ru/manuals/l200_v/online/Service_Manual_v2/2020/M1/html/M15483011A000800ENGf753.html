Diagnosis Item 6: Diagnose shorts in the power supply to CAN bus line (GW-CAN-C). <Vehicles with automatic A/C>
 
 
cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT CONDITIONS
The M.U.T.-III judges the trouble when it is impossible to receive the periodically sent data and the voltage of CAN_H or CAN_L line is more than 4.0 V.
 
 
TROUBLESHOOTING HINTS
  • Malfunction of the wiring harness
  • Malfunction of the connector
  • Malfunction of the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C)
  • Malfunction of the A/C-ECU
  • Malfunction of the camera-ECU <Vehicles with multi around monitor>
  • Malfunction of the multivision display <Vehicles with smartphone link display audio>
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the CAN bus lines. Voltage measurement at gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
noteThis inspection allows you to check that there is a short to power supply in either CAN_H line or CAN_L line. Thus, in the following steps, check the CAN bus line that is defective.
(1) Disconnect A/C-ECU connector and gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector, and measure the voltage at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector terminal No. 6 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector terminal No. 5 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
<Both of the measurement results show 4.0 V or less> Go to Step 10.
<Either of CAN_H line or CAN_L line measurement result shows more than 4.0 V> Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check the CAN line between joint connector (CAN3) and the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C). Voltage measurement at joint connector (CAN3).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
  • Strictly observe the specified wiring harness repair procedure.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN3), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 6 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 17 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
YES <Vehicles without multi around monitor and smartphone link display audio> : <4.0 V or less> Go to Step 9.
YES <Vehicles without multi around monitor and with smartphone link display audio> : <4.0 V or less> Go to Step 6.
YES <Vehicles with multi around monitor> : <4.0 V or less> Go to Step 3.
NO : <More than 4.0 V> Repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN3) and the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector.
 
STEP 3. Check the CAN line between joint connector (CAN3) and the camera-ECU. Voltage measurement at joint connector (CAN3).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN3), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 5 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 16 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
YES <Vehicles without smartphone link display audio> : <4.0 V or less> Go to Step 9.
YES <Vehicles with smartphone link display audio> : <4.0 V or less> Go to Step 6.
<More than 4.0 V> Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (camera-ECU connector disconnected)
(1) Disconnect camera-ECU connector, and diagnose by using the M.U.T.-III.
(2) Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen is as shown in the illustration.
Does M.U.T.-III screen correspond to the illustration?
Check the intermediate connector, and repair if necessary. If the intermediate connector is in good condition, repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN3) and the camera-ECU connector.
Check the camera-ECU connector, and repair if necessary. If the camera-ECU connector is in good condition, go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (retest the system)
Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen shows normal state.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the camera-ECU connector, and repair if necessary. If the camera-ECU connector is in good condition, replace the camera-ECU (Refer to GROUP 54A - Camera-ECU ).
 
STEP 6. Check the CAN line between joint connector (CAN3) and the multivision display. Voltage measurement at joint connector (CAN3).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN3), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 4 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 15 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
<4.0 V or less> Go to Step 9.
<More than 4.0 V> Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (multivision display connector disconnected)
(1) Disconnect multivision display connector, and diagnose by using the M.U.T.-III.
(2) Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen is as shown in the illustration.
Does M.U.T.-III screen correspond to the illustration?
Repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN3) and the multivision display connector.
Check the multivision display connector, and repair if necessary. If the multivision display connector is in good condition, go to Step 8.
 
STEP 8. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (retest the system)
Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen shows normal state.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the multivision display connector, and repair if necessary. If the multivision display connector is in good condition, replace the multivision display (Refer to GROUP 54A - Smartphone Link Display Audio ).
 
STEP 9. Check the CAN line between joint connector (CAN3) and the A/C-ECU. Voltage measurement at joint connector (CAN3).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
  • Strictly observe the specified wiring harness repair procedure.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN3), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 7 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between joint connector (CAN3) terminal No. 18 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
<4.0 V or less> Repair joint connector (CAN3).
NO : <More than 4.0 V> Repair the wiring harness between joint connector (CAN3) and the A/C-ECU connector.
 
STEP 10. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (A/C-ECU connector disconnected)
(1) Disconnect A/C-ECU connector and diagnose by using the M.U.T.-III.
(2) Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen is as shown in the illustration.
Does M.U.T.-III screen correspond to the illustration?
Go to Step 12.
Check the A/C-ECU connector, and repair if necessary. If the A/C-ECU connector is in good condition, go to Step 11.
 
STEP 11. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (retest the system)
Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen shows normal state.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the A/C-ECU connector, and repair if necessary. If the A/C-ECU connector is in good condition, replace the A/C-ECU (Refer to GROUP 55B - Heater Control Unit ).
 
STEP 12. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector disconnected)

(2) Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen is as shown in the illustration.

The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector, and repair if necessary. If the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector is in good condition, go to Step 13.
 
STEP 13. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics (retest the system)
Diagnose CAN bus lines, and check if M.U.T.-III screen shows normal state.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector, and repair if necessary. If the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) connector is in good condition, replace the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) (Refer to ).
