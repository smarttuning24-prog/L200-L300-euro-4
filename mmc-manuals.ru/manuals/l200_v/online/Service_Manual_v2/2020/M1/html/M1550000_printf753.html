GENERAL INFORMATION
 
 
FEATURES
 
 
IMPROVEMENTS IN COMFORT
  • By the adoption of a pollen filter, the air quality inside the passenger compartment has been improved.
 
 
Improvements in operation performance
  • Slide levers and rotary knobs have been used, thus improving ease of operation.
 
 
Improvements in fuel economy
  • The weight reduction of the HVAC system has been realised by structural rationalisation.
  • To optimise the air conditioner system, a compact and light-weight condenser has been used, thus saving fuel consumption.
  • High-performance five-vane rotary compressor has been adopted.
 
 
Improvements in safety
  • To defrost the door window glass quickly, ventilation air always blows towards the glass.
  • In order to protect the pedestrians, a nozzle defroster and an intake duct is designed to absorb the impact in an accident.
 
 
Global environment protection
HFC-134a (R134a) or HFO-1234yf (R1234yf) is used for refrigerant.
 
 
Service quality improvements
  • Incorporating of the condenser and receiver has reduced a possibility of refrigerant gas leakage and has increased serviceability.

INSPECTION PROCEDURE 1: Cool air does not come
 
 
 
 
CIRCUIT OPERATION
If cool air does not exit from the air outlets when the blower switch is on with the engine running, the A/C compressor and the fin thermo sensor circuits may be defective.
 
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • The refrigerant quantity is inadequate.
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the blower relay
  • Malfunction of the blower
  • Malfunction of the fin thermo sensor
  • Malfunction of the A/C switch
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU
  • Malfunction of the dual pressure switch
  • Malfunction of the A/C compressor relay
  • Malfunction of the A/C compressor and clutch
  • Malfunction of the heater controller assembly
  • Malfunction of the A/C fan control resistor

Check whether the engine-ECU sets any of the diagnosis codes below:

(1) Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.
(2) Air volume adjusting knob: Other than OFF

INSPECTION PROCEDURE 5: The PTC heater does not work <Vehicles with PTC heater>.
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
With the engine and blower motor being operated, if the PTC heater does not operate by setting the heater control temperature to MAX HOT position at the coolant temperature of 75 °C or lower, the PTC heater, PTC heater relay, heater controller assembly or its circuit system may be faulty.
 
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the PTC heater
  • Malfunction of the PTC heater relay 1
  • Malfunction of the PTC heater relay 2
  • Malfunction of the PTC heater relay 3
  • Damaged the wiring harness or connectors
  • Malfunction of the heater controller assembly
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU
 
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
1. Check the wiring harness between the heater controller assembly connector terminal 4 and engine-ECU.
2. Check the trouble symptom procedures of engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Inspection Procedure 26: PTC heater relay system )

1. Insert a thermometer into the air outlet located at the centre of instrument panel.
noteSet a thermometer so that cool air from the air outlet blows directly against the sensing part.
2. Set a wet and dry bulb thermometer in the air intake of the front passenger's foot area (below the glove box).

4. Set the A/C control panel to the mode instructed in the test conditions.
5. Wait until the air outlet temperature is stable (approximately 10 minutes after the A/C starts), and measure dry-bulb/wet-bulb temperatures at the air outlet and the air intake.
6. If the air outlet temperature is in the range specified by the table below, it is judged normal. REFILLING OF OIL IN THE A/C SYSTEM
Too little oil will provide inadequate compressor lubrication and cause a compressor failure. Too much oil will increase discharge air temperature.
When a compressor is installed at the factory, it contains 150 mL of compressor oil. While the A/C system is in operation, the oil is carried through the entire system by the refrigerant. Some of this oil will be trapped and retained in various parts of the system.
When the following system components are changed, it is necessary to add oil to the system to replace the oil being removed with the component.
Compressor oil: DH-PR <HFC-134a>, or YR20 or equivalent <HFO-1234yf>
Quantity
  • Evaporator: 35 mL
  • Condenser: 15 mL
  • A/C hose and pipe: 10 mL
