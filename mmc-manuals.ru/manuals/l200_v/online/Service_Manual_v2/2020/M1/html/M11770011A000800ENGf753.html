Code No. P0833 Clutch Interlock Switch Circuit <M/T>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The power voltage is supplied to the clutch interlock switch connector terminal from the J/B connector terminal.
  • The ON/OFF signal is inputted to the AS&G-ECU connector terminal CLTR from the clutch interlock switch connector terminal.
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The AS&G-ECU detects the state of the depressed clutch pedal by using the signal of the clutch interlock switch.
  • In response to the signal, the AS&G-ECU controls the AS&G system.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Condition
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position.
  • The diagnosis code U0100 is not detected.
Judgement Criterion
  • Although the driving is more than 30 km/h during the previous driving, the ON/OFF signal of the clutch interlock switch is not changed even once.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
In the normal operation, the auto stop is prohibited.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in clutch interlock switch circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed clutch interlock switch.
  • Failed installation of clutch interlock switch.
  • Failed clutch pedal.
  • Failed AS&G-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data lists (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 7: Clutch SW Bottom
OK:
  • ON (Clutch pedal: Fully depressed)
  • OFF (Clutch pedal: Released)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check the installation state of the clutch interlock switch and check the clutch pedal
  • Check the installation state of the clutch interlock switch.
  • Check whether the installation portion of the clutch interlock switch of the clutch pedal is not damaged.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace.
 
STEP 3. Check the clutch interlock switch itself
Check the clutch interlock switch itself (Refer to GROUP 21A - On-vehicles Service, Clutch Switch Check, Clutch Interlock Switch Continuity Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the clutch interlock switch.
 
STEP 4. Perform voltage measurement at clutch interlock switch connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Voltage between terminal IG1 line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair the connector(s), or check of short circuit to earth, and open circuit in IG1 line between J/B connector and clutch interlock switch connector.
 
STEP 5. Check short to earth, open circuit, and harness damage in CLTR line between clutch interlock switch connector and AS&G-ECU connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 6. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P0833 set?
Replace the AS&G-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
