INSPECTION PROCEDURE 1: Power windows do not work at all.
 
 
cautionBefore replacing the ECU, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If the power windows do not work at all, the power window relay or the ETACS-ECU may be defective.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the power window relay
  • Malfunction of ETACS-ECU
  • Damaged wiring harness and connectors
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the power supply system.
With the ignition switch in the "LOCK" (OFF) position, check if the following function operates normally:
  • Hazard warning lamp
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Refer to GROUP 54A, Malfunction of ETACS-ECU Power Supply Circuit .
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
Check that ETACS-ECU sets a diagnosis code.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Refer to GROUP 54A, Diagnosis Code Chart .
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. M.U.T.-III data list
Check the ETACS-ECU service item related to the power window.
 
Item No.Item nameNormal condition
30Ignition switch(IG1)ON
OK: Normal condition is displayed.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Refer to GROUP 54A, Inspection Procedure 2: The ignition switch (IG1) signal is not received .
 
STEP 4. Check the power window relay.
Refer to check the power window relay .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Replace the power window relay.
 
STEP 5. Voltage measurement at the power window relay connector (power supply line).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Check the voltage between the power window relay connector (power supply line) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in power supply line between power window relay connector and fusible link.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 7. Voltage measurement at the power window relay connector (B. PW line).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Check the voltage between the power window relay connector (B. PW line) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 8.
 
STEP 8. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in B. PW line between ETACS-ECU connector and power window relay connector.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Resistance measurement at the power window relay connector (earth line).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Continuity between power window relay connector (earth line) and body earth
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Go to Step 10.
 
STEP 10. Check of open circuit in earth line between power window relay connector and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 11. Retest the system.
Check that the all power windows work normally.
Is the check result normal?
The procedure is complete.
Replace the ETACS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 54A, ETACS - ETACS-ECU ).
