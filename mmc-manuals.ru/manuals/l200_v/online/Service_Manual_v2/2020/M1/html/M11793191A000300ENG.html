Inspection Procedure 1: Communication with No ECUs is Possible (ENG-LOCAL CAN).
 
 
cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction in ENG-LOCAL CAN (short to earth or power supply, wire short to one another)
  • Malfunction in the ECUs or sensors which are interconnected via ENG-LOCAL CAN (internal error) (DCU, Engine-ECU, No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, No. 2 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, exhaust muffler PM sensor)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the ENG-LOCAL CAN for short to power supply. Voltage measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Disconnect No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal CAN_H and body earth.
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal CAN_L and body earth.
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position and disconnect the negative battery terminal.
Is the check result normal?
YES <Voltage is 4.0 V or less in both CAN_H and CAN_L> : Go to Step 2.
NO <Voltage is more than 4.0 V in either CAN_H or CAN_L> : Check Inspection Procedure 2: Diagnose Shorts in The Power Supply to ENG-LOCAL CAN (Refer to ).
 
STEP 2. Check the ENG-LOCAL CAN for short to earth. Resistance measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(3) Disconnect No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(4) Resistance between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal CAN_H and body earth.
OK: 1 kΩ or more
(5) Resistance between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal CAN_L and body earth.
OK: 1 kΩ or more
Is the check result normal?
YES <Resistance value is 1 kΩ or more in both CAN_H and CAN_L> : Go to Step 3.
NO <Resistance value is less than 1 kΩ in either CAN_H or CAN_L> : Check Inspection Procedure 3: Diagnose Shorts in The Earth to ENG-LOCAL CAN (Refer to .
 
STEP 3. Check the ENG-LOCAL CAN for short to each other. Resistance measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(3) Resistance between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor harness side connector terminal CAN_H and CAN_L.
OK: 60 ± 10 Ω
(4) Connect No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
NO <Resistance value is less than 60 ± 10 Ω> : Check Inspection Procedure 4: Diagnose Shorts Between CAN_H and CAN_L Lines (ENG-LOCAL CAN) (Refer to ).
NO <Resistance value is 1 kΩ or more> : Check the terminal resistors in the engine-ECU and the DCU for open circuit. Then repair or replace if necessary.
 
STEP 4. Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor for short to earth. Resistance measurement at joint connector (CAN4).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(2) Disconnect joint connector (CAN4), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(3) Resistance between joint connector (CAN4) terminal No. 9 (CAN_H) and body earth.
OK: 1 kΩ or more
(4) Resistance between joint connector (CAN4) terminal No. 20 (CAN_L) and body earth.
OK: 1 kΩ or more
(5) Connect joint connector (CAN4) connector.
Is the check result normal?
YES <Resistance value is 1 kΩ or more in both CAN_H and CAN_L> : Go to Step 5.
NO <Resistance value is less than 1 kΩ in either CAN_H or CAN_L> : Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and repair if necessary. If the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector is in good condition, replace the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor (Refer to ).
 
STEP 5. Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor for short to power supply. Voltage measurement at joint connector (CAN4).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
  • The test wiring harness should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN4), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal, and turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Voltage between joint connector (CAN4) terminal No. 9 (CAN_H) and body earth.
OK: 4.0 V or less
(4) Voltage between joint connector (CAN4) terminal No. 20 (CAN_L) and body earth.
OK: 4.0 V or less
(5) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position and disconnect the negative battery terminal.
(6) Connect joint connector (CAN4).
Is the check result normal?
YES <Voltage is 4.0 V or less in both CAN_H and CAN_L> : Go to Step 6.
NO <Voltage is more than 4.0 V in either CAN_H or CAN_L> : Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and repair if necessary. If the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector is in good condition, replace the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor (Refer to ).
 
STEP 6. Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor for short to each other. Resistance measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector.
cautionA digital multimeter should be used (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
(1) Disconnect No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and measure at the component side.
(2) Resistance between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal CAN_H and CAN_L.
OK: 1 kΩ or more
Is the check result normal?
Check Inspection Procedure 5: DCU Power Supply System (Communication Between M.U.T.-III and DCU is Not Possible) (Refer to ).
Check the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector, and repair if necessary. If the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector is in good condition, replace the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor (Refer to ).
