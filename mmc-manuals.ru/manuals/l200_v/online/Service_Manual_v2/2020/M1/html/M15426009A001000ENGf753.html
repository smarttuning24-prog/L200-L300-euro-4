Inspection Procedure 1: The Rear Fog Lamp do not Illuminate Normally.
 
 
cautionWhenever the ECU is replaced, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If the rear fog lamps do not illuminate normally, the input signal circuit(s) below or the ETACS-ECU may be defective.
  • Tail lamp switch
  • Headlamp switch
  • Fog lamp switch
  • Option coding information
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the rear fog lamp do not illuminate normally, the mentioned input signal circuit(s) or ETACS-ECU may be defective.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the column switch
  • Malfunction of the ETACS-ECU
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. ETACS-ECU coding data check
(1) Operate the M.U.T.-III to read the ETACS-ECU option coding information.
(2) Check that the "Rear fog lamp" is set to "Present."
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Operate the M.U.T.-III to set the option coding "Rear fog lamp" to "Present," and check the trouble symptom.
 
STEP 2. Check the operation of the position lamps and the headlamps.
Check that the position lamps and the headlamps illuminate normally.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Check the position lamps and the headlamps (Refer to trouble symptom chart ).
 
STEP 3. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
Check if diagnosis code is set in the ETACS-ECU.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Troubleshoot the ETACS-ECU (Refer to ).
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III data list
Use the ETACS-ECU data list to check the signal related to the operation of rear fog lamp function.
  • Turn the fog lamp switch to ON.
 
Item No.Item nameNormal condition
71Rear fog lamp ON
OK: Normal condition is displayed.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Troubleshoot the ETACS-ECU. Refer to Inspection Procedure 9 "The column switch signal is not received" .
 
STEP 5. Check the rear fog lamp relay
Refer to .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the rear fog lamp relay.
 
STEP 6. Voltage measurement at the rear fog lamp relay connector
(1) Remove the rear fog lamp relay, and measure at the relay box side.
(2) Measure the voltage between the rear fog lamp relay connector (power supply terminal) and the body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in power supply lines between rear fog lamp relay connector and the fusible link.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 8. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in RFOG line between rear fog lamp relay connector and the ETACS-ECU connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Check the bulb of the rear combination lamp (rear fog lamp) <Vehicles with rear body>, rear fog lamp <Vehicles without rear body>.
Check the bulb of the defective lamp.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Replace the bulb of the defective lamp.
 
STEP 10. Check of short to power supply, and open circuit in output lines between rear fog lamp relay connector and the rear combination lamp (rear fog lamp) connector <Vehicles with rear body>, rear fog lamp connector <Vehicles without rear body>.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 11. Check of open circuit in body earth lines between the rear combination lamp (rear fog lamp) connector <Vehicles with rear body>, rear fog lamp connector <Vehicles without rear body> and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 12. Retest the system
Check that the front fog lamps illuminate normally.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the ETACS-ECU (Refer to ).
