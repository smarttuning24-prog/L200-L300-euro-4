Inspection Procedure 15: Overheating
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
The failure could possibly be caused by the failed engine cooling system, defective cooling fan system, faulty engine coolant temperature sensor and so on.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Insufficient amount or deteriorated quality engine coolant
  • Failed cooling fan system
  • Failed engine coolant temperature sensor
  • Failed thermostat
  • Failed water pump
  • Failed radiator core
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the diagnosis code.
Is any diagnosis code set?
Inspection chart for diagnosis code (Refer to ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check the cooling fan
Check the cooling fan.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair.
 
STEP 3. Check engine coolant
noteWhen the engine coolant level is too low, check for any section that cases leakage and repair, if necessary.
Check engine coolant (Refer to GROUP 14 - On-vehicle Service, Engine Coolant Leak Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace or fill the engine coolant.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 7: Engine coolant temperature sensor
OK:
  • Engine cold state: At ambient temperature (atmospheric temperature) or equivalent.
  • Engine hot state: At 80 to 120°C
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Check engine coolant temperature sensor system (Refer to Diagnosis code No. P0117 or No. P0118 ).
 
STEP 5. Check thermostat
Check thermostat (Refer to GROUP 14 - Thermostat, Inspection ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the thermostat.
 
STEP 6. Check water pump
Check water pump.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Replace the water pump.
 
STEP 7. Check the trouble symptom
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the radiator.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
