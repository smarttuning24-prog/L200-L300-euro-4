Inspection Procedure 6: Improper Starting (Long Time to Start) <4N15>
 
 
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed battery
  • Improper fuel used
  • Fuel contaminated with foreign matter
  • Injector leaking or clogged
  • Failed fuel injector
  • Fuel filter clogged
  • Fuel line leaking or clogged
  • Failed fuel pressure sensor
  • Failed supply pump (suction control valve)
  • Failed glow system
  • Failed EGR system operation
  • Failed engine-ECU
  • Compression pressure low
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check battery
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery, On-vehicle Service, Battery Test ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the battery.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the diagnosis code.
Is any diagnosis code set?
Inspection chart for diagnosis code (Refer to ).
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data lists (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 5: Barometric pressure sensor
  • Item No. 7: Engine coolant temperature sensor
  • Item No. 8: EGR gas temperature sensor
  • Item No. 16: EGR valve position sensor
  • Item No. 21: Fuel temperature sensor
  • Item No. 43: Intake air temperature sensor
Are the check results normal?
Go to Step 4.
Perform the diagnosis code classified check procedure for the sensor that has shown an abnormal data value (Refer to ).
 
STEP 4. Check the using fuel
Check for foreign matters (water, petrol fuel, etc.) in fuel.
Is the specified fuel being used?
Go to Step 5.
Replace the fuel.
 
STEP 5. M.U.T.-III actuator test
Use M.U.T.-III to perform the actuator test (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 54: Glow (PWM) 0%
  • Item No. 55: Glow (PWM) 50%
  • Item No. 56: Glow (PWM) 100%
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Check the glow plug system (Refer to Diagnosis code No. P0381: Glow Plug Open/Short Malfunction ).
 
STEP 6. Check fuel supply line
  • Check for fuel leakage and clogging.
  • Check fuel filter for clogging.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair.
 
STEP 7. M.U.T.-III actuator test
Use M.U.T.-III to perform the actuator test (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 19: Suction control valve
OK: The suction control valve of supply pump is driven
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Check suction control valve system (Refer to Diagnosis code No. P0003 or No. P0004 ).
 
STEP 8. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data lists (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 63: Rail pressure sensor
  • Item No. 64: Rail pressure target
Are the check results normal?
Go to Step 11.
Go to Step 9.
 
STEP 9. Replace the fuel pressure sensor
After replacing the common-rail assembly, re-check the trouble symptoms.
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Go to Step 10.
Check end.
 
STEP 10. Check the trouble symptoms
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the supply pump assembly.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
 
STEP 11. Check compression pressure
Check compression pressure (Refer to GROUP 11A - On-vehicle Service, Compression Pressure Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Repair.
 
STEP 12. Check fuel injector itself
  • Fuel injector connector check.
  • Check fuel injector itself (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the fuel injector at the failed cylinder.
 
STEP 13. Check the trouble symptoms
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the engine-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
