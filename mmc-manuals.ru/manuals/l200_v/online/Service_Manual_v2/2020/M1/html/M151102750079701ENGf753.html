51 -EXTERIOR > HEADLAMP WASHER > TROUBLESHOOTING > HEADLAMP WASHER DOES NOT WORK NORMALLY
Inspection Procedure: The headlamp washer does not work.
 
 
cautionWhenever the ECU is replaced, ensure that the input and output signal circuits are normal.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
The headlamp washer motor, the headlamp washer relay, the column switch or the ETACS-ECU may be defective.
 
 
 
POSSIBLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the headlamp washer motor
  • Malfunction of the headlamp washer relay
  • Malfunction of the column switch
  • Malfunction of the ETACS-ECU
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
 
 
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code.
When the ignition switch is turned to the LOCK (OFF) position, check that the ETACS-ECU does not set the diagnosis code.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Refer to GROUP 54A, Diagnosis Code Chart .
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list
Check the input signals below which are related to the headlamp washer.
  • Ignition switch: ON
  • Headlamp washer switch: ON
 
Item No.Item nameNormal condition
30Ignition switch (IG1)ON
64Headlamp washer outputON
OK: Normal conditions are displayed for all the items.
Is the check result normal?
All the signals are received normally. : Go to Step 3.
The ignition switch (IG1) signal is not received. : Refer to GROUP 54A, Input signal procedure 2 "The ignition switch (IG1) signal is not received ".
The headlamp washer switch signal is not received. : Refer to GROUP 54A, Input signal procedure 9 "The column switch signal is not received ".
 
STEP 3. Check the headlamp washer motor assembly.
Refer to .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the headlamp washer motor.
 
STEP 4. Resistance measurement at headlamp washer motor connector (earth terminal).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Continuity between headlamp washer motor connector (earth terminal) and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. Check of open circuit in earth line between headlamp washer motor connector and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 6. Check the headlamp washer relay.
Refer to .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Replace the headlamp washer relay.
 
STEP 7. Voltage measurement at headlamp washer relay connector (power supply line).
(1) Remove the headlamp washer relay, and measure at wiring harness side.
(2) Check the voltage between headlamp washer relay connector (power supply terminals) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 8.
 
STEP 8. Check of the open circuit in power supply line between headlamp washer relay connector and the fusible link.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Check of the open circuit in HWA line between ETACS-ECU connector and headlamp washer relay connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 10. Check of the open circuit in power supply line between headlamp washer motor connector and headlamp washer relay connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 11. Retest the system.
The headlamp washer should now work normally.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the ETACS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 54A, ETACS - ETACS-ECU ).
