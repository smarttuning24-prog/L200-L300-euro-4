Code No. U029D: Lost Communication with No. 1 NOx Sensor
 
 
cautionReplace No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor after certainly confirming that the communication circuits are normal.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position.
  • DCU supply voltage is more than 10 V.
  • After 1 second have passed since the DCU was started.
Judgement Criterion
  • Unable to receive No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor signals through the CAN bus line.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
Current malfunction
  • The failure is possibly caused by malfunction of the harness and connector in CAN bus line between DCU and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, by malfunction in the power supply system of No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor, in No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor itself, in DCU.
Past malfunction
  • Proceed with troubleshooting focusing on malfunction of the harness and connector in CAN bus line between DCU and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor and malfunction in the power supply system of No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor. For diagnosis procedure, refer to how to cope with past malfunctions. (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Treat Past Trouble ).
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor power supply circuit
  • Failed harness and connector in CAN bus line
  • Failed harness and connector in earth line (Between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal No. 5 and body earth)
  • Failed No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Perform voltage measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between power supply terminal (terminal No. 1) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of short to earth and open circuit in power supply line between engine control relay connector and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector (terminal No. 1).
 
STEP 2. Perform resistance measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between earth terminal (terminal No. 2) and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of open circuit and damage in earth line between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector (terminal No. 2) and body earth.
 
STEP 3. Perform resistance measurement at No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between earth terminal (terminal No. 5) and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of open circuit and damage in earth line between No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector (terminal No. 5) and body earth.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between DCU connector terminal CANH and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal
cautionStrictly observe the specified wiring harness repair procedure (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
Check CANH line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between DCU connector terminal CANL and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor connector terminal
cautionStrictly observe the specified wiring harness repair procedure (For details refer to GROUP 54C - Service Precautions ).
Check CANL line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. U029D set?
Replace the No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor (Refer to ).
Intermittent malfunction in CAN bus line between DCU and No. 1 exhaust muffler NOx sensor (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
