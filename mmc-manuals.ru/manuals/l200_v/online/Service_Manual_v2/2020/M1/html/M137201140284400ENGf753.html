REMOVAL SERVICE POINTS
 
 
<<A>> STEERING WHEEL ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
1. Position the steering wheel in a straight ahead position.
2.
cautionUse the special tool to remove the steering wheel since the steering column collision adsorbing mechanism may be damaged.
Use special tool steering wheel puller (MB990803) to remove the steering wheel.
 

 
 
<<B>> STEERING WHEEL BEZEL (LH/RH)/STEERING WHEEL REMOTE CONTROL SWITCH (LH/RH)
cautionTo remove the steering wheel bezel or the steering wheel remote control switch, remove the left or right side only.If fail to observe this, the steering lower cover will fall down, thus damaging it.

 
 
<<C>> HEATED STEERING WHEEL-ECU REMOVAL
cautionRemove only the left side of the steering wheel bezel or the steering wheel remote control switch.If fail to observe this, the steering lower cover will fall down, thus damaging it.
Loosen the tapping screws behind the steering wheel, and then slide the steering wheel lower cover away from the steering wheel to allow sufficient clearance to remove the ECU mounting screws.
 
 
<<D>> HEATER ELEMENT CONNECTOR CONNECTION REMOVAL
caution
  • To remove the heated steering wheel ECU, disconnect the heater element connector carefully to avoid damage to the heater element.
  • Do not remove the lower cover completely as the heater element is adhered to the lower cover by using an adheseive tape.Also be careful not to peel off the adhesive tape.
 
 
 
INSTALLATION SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< HEATER ELEMENT CONNECTOR CONNECTION INSTALLATION
cautionTo installing the heated steering wheel ECU, reconnect the heater element connector carefully to avoid damage to the heater element.
 
 
>>B<< HEATED STEERING WHEEL-ECU WIRING HARNESS/STEERING WHEEL REMOTE CONTROL SWITCH (LH/RH) WIRING HARNESS INSTALLATION
Route the heated steering wheel-ECU wiring harness and the steering remote control switch (LH/RH) wiring harness as shown.
 

 
 
>>C<< STEERING WHEEL ASSEMBLY/DRIVER'S AIR BAG MODULE INSTALLATION
1.
cautionWhen installing the steering wheel assembly and air bag module, do not trap the clock spring harness.
Perform the clock spring centre alignment (Refer to 52B - Driver’s Air Bag Module and Clock Spring ).
2. After checking that the clock spring centre alignment is already performed, install the steering wheel assembly so that its boss part is aligned with the rib of the clock spring slowly.
3. Install the driver's airbag module.
4. After the installation, check that there is no abnormality when the steering wheel is fully turned to left and right.
