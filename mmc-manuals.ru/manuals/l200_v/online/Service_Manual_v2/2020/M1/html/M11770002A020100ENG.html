GENERAL INFORMATION
 
 
AUTO STOP & GO (AS&G) SYSTEM
The purpose of the Auto Stop & Go (AS&G) system is to reduce CO2 exhaust gases from vehicles. The AS&G system automatically should stop the engine when the vehicle is stopped. And also the system should automatically restart the engine when the vehicle is started. The AS&G-ECU judges whether the engine should be stopped or restarted. The AS&G-ECU also stops or restarts the engine. The AS&G-ECU monitors the vehicle condition by using the signals from sensors and each ECU. If the specified conditions are satisfied, the AS&G-ECU should send the engine stop request signal to the engine-ECU via CAN. When receiving the signal, the engine-ECU should stop the engine (auto stop). When the driver depresses the clutch pedal during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should turn on the starter relay and the starter cut relay to drive the starter, also should send the engine start request signal to the engine-ECU via CAN bus line <M/T>. When receiving the signal, the engine-ECU should stop the engine (auto stop). When the driver release the brake pedal during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should turn on the starter relay to drive the starter, also should send the engine start request signal to the engine-ECU via CAN bus line <A/T>. When receiving the signal, the engine-ECU should restart the engine (auto start).

1. Auto Stop & Go (AS&G) System
When the ignition switch is turned "ON" position or when the engine starts, the AS&G system should automatically be turned ON. The driver can turn OFF the AS&G system by pressing the AS&G OFF switch. At that time, the AS&G OFF display screen should be turned ON in the multi-information display of the combination meter. When the AS&G OFF switch is pressed again, the AS&G system should be turned ON.
 

 
 
2. Auto Stop Control
The engine-ECU will stop the engine when the following operation is performed while the AS&G display screen illuminates in the multi-information display of the combination meter.
 
 
 
<M/T>
  1. While letting the vehicle run and perfectly stop, depress the clutch pedal to place the gearshift lever in the neutral position.
  2. Release the clutch pedal.
During the auto stop, the AS&G indicator is turned ON in the combination meter. When one or more of following conditions are satisfied, the AS&G-ECU should prohibit the auto stop.
  • After the key is used to start the engine, approximately 30 seconds have not been taken.
  • The accelerator pedal is depressed.
  • If the vehicle is stopped again within approximately 10 seconds of the engine auto start.
  • More than the vehicle speed of approximately 5 km/h is not detected even once after the engine start, including the engine start of the auto start.
  • The auto stop prohibition signal is received from the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - General Information ).
  • The auto stop prohibition signal is received from the A/C-ECU.
  • The auto stop prohibition signal is received from the transfer-ECU <4WD>.
  • The vacuum pressure of the brake booster is less than specified value.
  • The engine-ECU detects an abnormality in the AS&G system.
  • The AS&G-ECU detects an abnormality in the AS&G system.
  • The driver's seat belt is unfastened.
  • The hood is opened.
  • The driver’s door is opened.
  • The ambient temperature is lower than approximately 0°C
  • The steering wheel is being steered.
  • Parking on steep hill.
 
 
<A/T>
  1. Depress the brake pedal when the vehicle is running to stop the vehicle ("D" range transmission).
  2. If the brake pedal is depressed continuously when the vehicle has completely stopped, the engine will automatically stop.
During the auto stop, the AS&G display screen should be turned ON in the multi-information display of the combination meter. When one or more of following conditions are satisfied, the AS&G-ECU should prohibit the auto stop.
  • After the key/engine switch is used to start the engine, approximately 30 seconds have not been taken.
  • The accelerator pedal is depressed.
  • If the vehicle is stopped again within approximately 10 seconds of the engine auto start.
  • More than the vehicle speed of approximately 5 km/h is not detected even once after the engine start, including the engine start of the auto start.
  • The vacuum pressure of the brake booster is less than specified value.
  • The brake fluid pressure is less than the specified value.
  • The driver's seat belt is unfastened.
  • The hood is opened.
  • The driver’s door is opened.
  • The parking brakes are applied.
  • The steering wheel is being steered.
  • The vehicle slant angle is more than specified value.
  • The ambient temperature is lower than approximately 0°C.
  • The AS&G-ECU detects an abnormality in the AS&G system.
  • The auto stop prohibition signal is received from each ECU with the conditions described in the following table.

3. Auto Start Control
cautionIf the following motions are carried out during the auto stop even one time, the AS&G-ECU should blink the AS&G display screen in the multi-information display of the combination meter and prohibits the auto start. Even if trying to return to the previous status after that, the auto start should be prohibited. When restarted, the engine must be started by the key/engine switch.
  • In case the hood is opened
noteWhen in the states mentioned above, the AS&G-ECU should send the signal to the ETACS-ECU via the CAN to sound the warning buzzer built into the ETACS-ECU. This warns the driver that this is different from normal use.
 
 
Normal auto start <M/T>
When the driver depresses the clutch pedal during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should turn on the starter relay and the starter cut relay to drive the starter, also should send the engine start request signal to the engine-ECU via CAN bus line. When receiving the signal, the engine-ECU should restart the engine. When the engine starts, the AS&G display screen in the multi-information display of the combination meter should go off. When the following conditions are satisfied during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should prohibit the auto start.
  • AS&G system: OFF*
  • Gearshift lever position: except neutral position
note*: Pressing the AS&G OFF switch again to turn ON the AS&G system can enable the auto start.
 
 
Normal auto start <A/T>
Depending on the driver's operation, the AS&G-ECU turns off the AS&G display screen that was displayed in the multi-information display of the combination meter while quickly starting the engine. Auto start should function either by brake pedal or accelerator pedal operation. Auto start conditions are as follows:

  • If the selector lever is moved from "D" position to outside "D" position, and then back to "D" position again, the engine will the engine will perform auto start.
  • *: The engine does not start automatically even if the brake pedal is released.
 
 
Forcible auto start <M/T>
When one of the following conditions is satisfied during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should send the engine start request signal to the engine-ECU for starting the engine swiftly despite the driver's determination. Also, the AS&G-ECU should turn on the starter relay and the starter cut relay to drive the starter. At that time, the AS&G display screen in the multi-information display of the combination meter should go off as well as the normal auto start.
  • The vehicle speed is more than 3 km/h.
  • The steering wheel is being steered.
  • The vacuum pressure of the brake booster is less than specified value.
  • The engine start request signal is received from the A/C-ECU.
  • The engine start request signal is received from the engine-ECU.
  • The AS&G-ECU detects an abnormality in the AS&G system.
  • The driver's seat belt is unfastened.
  • The driver's door is opened.
  • HDC switch is turned on.
When the following conditions are satisfied during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should prohibit the forcible auto start.
  • AS&G system: OFF*
  • Gearshift lever position: except neutral position
note*: Pressing the AS&G OFF switch again to turn ON the AS&G system can enable the forcible auto start.

4. Control for Increasing Voltage
At the auto start, the AS&G-ECU sends the increasing voltage request signal to the DC/DC converter (entertainment system). When receiving the signal, the DC/DC converter (entertainment system) should temporarily switch the relay in the unit to the voltage converter of the DC/DC converter (entertainment system). The voltage converter increases the battery voltage dropping during cranking to 12.2 V. This can prevent the jumpy sound of the audio equipment due to the battery voltage drop at the auto start. The DC/DC converter (entertainment system) should stop increasing the voltage when the input voltage returns to 12.2 V. There is a DC/DC converter (entertainment system) in both the control system and the entertainment system. The control system controls the power supply voltage of the A/T-ECU and ASTC-ECU. <A/T> The entertainment system controls the power voltage supply of Mitsubishi Multi Communication System (MMCS), audio, etc.

6. Starter Relay Control <M/T>
At normal engine start
  • When the following signals are input, the AS&G-ECU should turn ON the both of the power transistor of the starter cut relay in the unit and the power transistor of the starter relay in the unit. This allows the starter cut relay and the starter relay to be turned ON, and then the starter is driven. If the immobilizer result checking has some discrepancy, the output to the starter relay should be turned OFF. The starter, therefore, should not be driven.
    • Ignition switch-"ST" signal
    • Clutch interlock switch ON signal (the clutch pedal is fully depressed)
At engine start from auto stop status
  • When detecting one of the following conditions during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should turn ON the both of the power transistor of the starter cut relay in the unit and the power transistor of the starter relay in the unit. This allows the starter cut relay and the starter relay to be turned ON, and then the starter is driven.
    • Neutral position switch: ON; Clutch monitoring switch: ON (the clutch pedal is depressed.)
    • Neutral position switch: ON and the conditions of the forcible auto start are satisfied
 
 
6. Starter Relay Control <A/T>
At normal engine start
  • When the following signals are input, the AS&G-ECU should turn ON the power transistor of the starter relay in the unit. This allows the starter relay to be turned ON, and then the starter is driven. If the immobilizer result checking has some discrepancy, the output to the starter relay should be turned OFF. The starter, therefore, should not be driven.
    • Ignition switch-ST signal
    • Transmission: "P" or "N" position
At engine start from auto stop status
  • When detecting the conditions for auto start during the auto stop, the AS&G-ECU should turn ON the power transistor of the starter relay in the unit. This allows the starter relay to be turned ON, and then the starter is driven.
