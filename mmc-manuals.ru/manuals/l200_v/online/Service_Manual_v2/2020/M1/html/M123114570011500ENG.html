Inspection Procedure 41: A/T fluid temperature warning lamp or A/T fluid temperature warning display on multi-information display flashes or does not go out
 
 
 
 
cautionWhenever the ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If the A/T fluid temperature warning display on multi-information display flashes or does not go out, the cause is probably a malfunction of the A/T fluid temperature sensor 1 system, A/T fluid temperature sensor 2 system or A/T-ECU.
noteAs the cause for the A/T fluid temperature warning lamp or A/T fluid temperature warning display on multi-information display to flash or not to go out, a combination meter malfunction and combination meter circuit malfunction are suspected. However, it shall be presupposed that no malfunction is present to the combination meter system.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the CAN bus line
  • Malfunction of the transmission wiring harness (A/T fluid temperature sensors 1 and 2)
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check of fluid level check mode
Turn the ignition switch from the "LOCK" (OFF) to "ON" position, and check again if the A/T fluid temperature warning lamp illuminates or flashes.
noteWhen the mode is in the fluid check mode (fluid level check mode of A/T Fluid Check <M.U.T.-III not used>), the A/T fluid temperature warning lamp or A/T fluid temperature warning display on multi-information display illuminates or flashes. To cancel this status, turn the ignition switch from the "LOCK" (OFF) to "ON" position.
Does the A/T fluid temperature warning lamp or A/T fluid temperature warning display on multi-information display illuminate or flash?
Go to Step 2.
The procedure is complete.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnostics
Using M.U.T.-III, perform CAN bus line diagnosis.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair the CAN bus line (Refer to GROUP 54C - Troubleshooting ), and then go to Step 4.
 
STEP 3. Check the A/T fluid temperature sensor 1 and A/T fluid temperature sensor 2.
Refer to .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the transmission wiring harness (A/T fluid temperature sensors 1 and 2).
 
STEP 4. Retest the system.
Is the trouble symptom reproduced?
Replace the A/T-ECU.
The procedure is complete.
