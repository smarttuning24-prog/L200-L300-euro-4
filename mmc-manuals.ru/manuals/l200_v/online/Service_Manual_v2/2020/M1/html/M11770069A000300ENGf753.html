Inspection Procedure 11: DC/DC Converter (Control System) Power Supply Circuit <A/T>
 
 
cautionEven when diagnosis code No. B1351 or B1352 is set, do not conclude as a DC/DC converter (control system) or AS&G-ECU error, and be sure to check the DC/DC converter (control system) power supply circuit.
 
 
OPERATION
  • The power voltage is supplied to the DC/DC converter (control system) connector terminal PWR from the fusible link.
  • The ignition switch-IG1 signal is inputted into the DC/DC converter (control system) connector terminal IGN from the ignition switch (IG1) connector terminal <vehicles without KOS> or IG1 relay connector terminal. <vehicles with KOS>
  • The DC/DC converter (control system) connector terminal GND is earthed to the vehicle body.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in power supply circuit, or loose connector contact.
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in ignition switch-IG1 circuit, or loose connector contact. <vehicles without KOS>
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in IG1 relay circuit, or loose connector contact. <vehicles with KOS>
  • Open circuit or harness damage in DC/DC converter (control system) earth circuit, or loose connector contact.
  • Failed DC/DC converter (control system).
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Perform voltage measurement at DC/DC converter (control system) connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Voltage between terminal DC/DC converter (control system) PWR line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
  • Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of short to body earth and open circuit in PWR line between fusible link and DC/DC converter (control system) connector.
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at DC/DC converter (control system) connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal DC/DC converter (control system) IGN line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
YES <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 6.
YES <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 7.
NO <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 3.
NO <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 3. Check ignition switch itself <Vehicles without KOS>
Check ignition switch itself (Refer to GROUP 54A - Ignition switch, Inspection, Ignition Switch Check ).
Is the check result normal?
  • Repair or replace the connector(s), or check and repair the harness of short to body earth, and open circuit in IGN line between ignition switch connector and DC/DC converter (control system) connector.
Replace the ignition switch.
 
STEP 4. Check IG1 relay itself <Vehicles with KOS>
Check IG1 relay itself (Refer to GROUP 42B - On-vehicle Service, Relay Inspection ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Replace the IG1 relay.
 
STEP 5. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in IG1 line between IG1 relay connector and DC/DC converter (control system) connector <Vehicles with KOS>
Is the check result normal?
Perform the troubleshooting of the KOS&OSS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 42B - Troubleshooting, Symptom Chart ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 6. Check of harness damage in IGN line between ignition switch connector and DC/DC converter (control system) connector <Vehicles without KOS>
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 7. Check of harness damage in IGN line between IG1 relay connector and DC/DC converter (control system) connector <Vehicles with KOS>
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 8. Perform resistance measurement at DC/DC converter (control system) connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between terminal DC/DC converter (control system) GND line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
  • Repair the connector(s), or check and repair of open circuit and harness damage in GND line between DC/DC converter (control system) connector and body earth.
 
STEP 9. Check of harness damage in PWR line between fusible link and DC/DC converter (control system) connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 10. Check the trouble symptom
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the DC/DC converter (control system).
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
