Fabricating a reflector for inspection of the BSW
1. Obtain an aluminium plate and the tools below:
What to obtain
  1. Aluminium plate: 300 mm (W), 150 mm (H) and 0.5 mm (D) or more
    noteTo fabricate the plate easily, we recommend that the plate should be 0.5 mm thick. If thinner, the plate may be curved easily. If thicker, it will take longer to fabricate the plate.
  2. Protective gloves
  3. 300-mm long scale
  4. Knife
  5. Adhesive tape
2. Wear protective gloves and scribe the lines on the aluminium plate as shown.
note
  • Check the scribed lines for accuracy (parallelism and perpendicularity) in several points.
  • Place cloths under the plate to protect it from scratches.
  • When the plate is covered with a protective film at the factory, do not peel off it.
3. Cut the plate according to the steps below:
(1) Align a scale along the scribed line.
(2) Hold the scale securely. Use it as a guide to make a V-groove on the plate with a knife.
(3) Repeat the process above approximately 20 times to deepen the V-groove.
(4) Bend the plate slightly to check that the plate can be bent sharply along the V-groove as shown. If no, the V-groove is too shallow. Repeat step (3).
(5) Bend the plate several times to cut it.

4. Assemble the plate pieces according to the steps below to make a triangular pyramid.
(1) Use 3 of 4 plate pieces. If the piece is covered with a protective film, do not peel off it yet.
(2) Place the plate pieces and apply adhesive strips together as shown. When the plate pieces are covered with a protective film, apply the strips on the non-covered surface. (Peel off only one side of films when both sides of the plate pieces are protected)
noteTo make a triangular pyramid, clearance between the plates should be 0.5 to 1.0 mm.
(3) Apply an adhesive strip on the edges to assemble the triangular pyramid as shown.
note
  • Apply adhesive strips on the outside of the panel as the inside reflects the radar wave.
  • When protective films are applied on the inside, peel off them.
Complete
 
 
 
Locating the BSW reflector
caution
To carry out an accurate inspection, keep away any metal obstacles other than a bench (e.g. garage jack) where the reflector is placed from the reflector as shown.
1. Draw line (a) from the centre of the Three-diamond mark on the front and rear ends (line a: vehicle centre line).
2. Draw line (b) from the centre of the Three-diamond mark on the rear wheel (line b: rear axle centre line).
3. The bottom of the reflector should be positioned 2,060 ± 30 mm from the vehicle centre line and 2,871 ± 30 mm from the rear axle centre line.
4.
The top of reflector should be 680 ± 20 mm away from the floor and the bottom should be parallel to the BSW-ECU as shown.
noteEnsure that the tyre pressures are correct and the vehicle is parked on a flat level at kerbside weight.
 
 
 
Check process
1. Connect the M.U.T.-III.
2. Ensure that the BSW system is turned on. If no, operate the blind spot warning switch to turn on the system.
3. Proceed to the steps below on the M.U.T.-III:
(1) Select "BSW" or "BSW(SUB)."
noteSelect "BSW" for the BSW-ECU (master) and "BSW(SUB)" for the BSW-ECU (slave), respectively.
(2) Select "Special function."
(3) Select "Test."
(4) Select "Radar recognition angle inspection."
4. Observe the flow chart below:
noteIf not within the specified value, any of the causes below is suspected.
  • The BSW-ECU is defective.
  • The BSW-ECU bracket is defective (deformed)
  • The BSW-ECU bracket mount is defective (deformed)
  • Damaged bumper (scratches or dents)
 
 
 
Radar recognition angle
The illustration shows a radar recognition angle.
noteWhen the reflector is positioned straight against the BSW-ECU, "0°" should be shown. If the BSW-ECU is swayed clockwise, a positive value will be shown. If anti-clockwise, a negative value will be shown.
