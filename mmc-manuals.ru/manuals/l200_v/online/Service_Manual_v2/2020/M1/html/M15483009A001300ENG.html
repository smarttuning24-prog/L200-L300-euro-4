Diagnosis Item 4: Diagnose shorts between CAN_H and L lines (CAN-C). <RHD>
 
 
cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
When all of the following conditions are met, the M.U.T.-III determines that a short circuit is present between CAN_L and CAN_H lines.
  • The M.U.T.-III cannot receive the periodic transmission data from any ECUs.
  • The voltage between CAN bus lines (CAN_L and CAN_H lines) is 1.0 V or more, 4.0 V or less.
  • The resistance at CAN bus line (CAN_L and CAN_H lines) is 2 Ω or less.
 
 
TROUBLESHOOTING HINTS
  • Malfunction of the wiring harness
  • Malfunction of the connector
  • Malfunction of the ETACS-ECU
  • Malfunction of the combination meter
  • Malfunction of the transfer-ECU <4WD>
  • Malfunction of the KOS&OSS-ECU <Vehicles with KOS>
  • Malfunction of the SRS-ECU
  • Malfunction of the ASTC-ECU
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU <A/T>
  • Malfunction of the AS&G-ECU <Vehicles with AS&G system>
  • Malfunction of the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU <Vehicles with FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU>
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU
  • Malfunction of the steering wheel sensor (column switch assembly)
  • Malfunction of the clearance warning control unit <Vehicles with corner sensor system>
  • Malfunction of the gateway-ECU (for BSW) <Vehicles with Blind Spot Warning [BSW] (with Lane Change Assist) system>
  • Malfunction of the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) <Vehicles with gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C)>
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Connector check: joint connector (CAN2)
cautionThe strand end of the twist wire should be within 10 cm from the connector.
When checking the joint connector, ensure that its wiring harness side and its short pins are not damaged.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair the defective connector.
 
STEP 2. Resistance measurement at joint connector (CAN2).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN2), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(4) Resistance between joint connector (CAN2) terminal Nos. 10 and 21
OK: 120 ± 20 Ω
Is the check result normal?
<Within 120 ± 20 Ω> Go to Step 16.
<Not within 120 ± 20 Ω> Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Connector check: joint connector (CAN1)
cautionThe strand end of the twist wire should be within 10 cm from the connector.
When checking the joint connector, ensure that its wiring harness side and its short pins are not damaged.
Is the check result normal?
