Code No. P0381: Glow Plug Open/Short Malfunction
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The electrical current source is supplied from the engine control relay connector terminal to the glow control unit connector terminal B. Also, it is supplied from the glow control unit connector terminal GND to the body earth.
  • The request signal of operating the glow plug is input into the glow control unit connector terminal G/RL from the engine-ECU connector terminal G/CT.
  • The voltage for the glow plug is applied from the battery to the glow control unit connector terminal GLWB.
  • The voltage is applied from the glow control unit connector terminal GLW1, GLW2, GLW3 and GLW4 to each glow plug.
  • The diagnosis signal is output from the glow control unit connector terminal DIAG to the engine-ECU connector terminal G/DA.
 
 
FUNCTION
The voltage applied to the glow control unit from the battery is applied to each glow plug in the duty ratio according to the signal from the engine-ECU.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • While the engine is running
  • The electrical continuity duty to the glow plug is 17 to 83%.
Judgement Criterion
  • The information that the glow is abnormal is received from the glow control unit.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The AS&G control is prohibited.
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed glow plug
  • Failed glow control unit
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in glow plug circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Connector check: glow control unit connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair or replace the connector.
 
STEP 2. Connector check: No. 1 glow plug connector, No. 2 glow plug connector, No. 3 glow plug connector and No. 4 glow plug connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair or replace the connector.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between glow control unit connector terminal GLW1 and No. 1 glow plug connector terminal
noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connector B-07, and repair if necessary.
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between glow control unit connector terminal GLW2 and No. 2 glow plug connector terminal
noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connector B-07, and repair if necessary.
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between glow control unit connector terminal GLW3 and No. 3 glow plug connector terminal
noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connector B-07, and repair if necessary.
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Check harness between glow control unit connector terminal GLW4 and No.4 glow plug connector terminal
noteBefore checking harness, check intermediate connector B-07, and repair if necessary.
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 7. Check glow plug itself
Check glow plug itself (Refer to GROUP 16 - Glow System, On-vehicle Service, Glow plug Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Replace the glow plug (Refer to GROUP 16 - Glow System, Glow plug ).
 
STEP 8. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to perform the actuator test (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 55: Glow (PWM) 50%
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P0381 set?
Replace the glow control unit. Then go to Step 9.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
 
STEP 9. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to perform the actuator test (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 55: Glow (PWM) 50%
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P0381 set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
The check is end.
