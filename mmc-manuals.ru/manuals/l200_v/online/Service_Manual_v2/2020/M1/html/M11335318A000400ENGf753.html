Code No. P2453: Exhaust Differential Pressure Sensor Range/Performance Problem
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • A power voltage of 5 V is applied to the exhaust differential pressure sensor power terminal from the engine-ECU connector terminal DDP5 and earthed to the engine-ECU connector terminal DDPE from the exhaust differential pressure sensor connector terminal.
  • The sensor signal is inputted to the engine-ECU connector terminal DDPS from the exhaust differential pressure sensor output terminal.
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The exhaust differential pressure sensor outputs the voltage to the engine-ECU in accordance with the difference in the voltage between the DPF upstream area and the DPF downstream area.
  • The engine-ECU anticipates the amount of PM accumulated in the DPF through this output voltage.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V.
  • Vehicle in steady running.
Judgement Criterion
  • The exhaust differential pressure sensor output voltage is less than threshold value based on exhaust gas flow for predetermined time.
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V.
  • Predetermined time has passed after the engine is stopped.
  • The malfunction of diagnosis code No. P2454 or No. P2455 is not detected.
Judgement Criterion
  • The exhaust differential pressure sensor output is more than 2.75 kPa for predetermined time.
or
  • The exhaust differential pressure sensor output is less than -2.75 kPa for predetermined time.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The EGR control is stopped (Fully closed).
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • The exhaust pressure hose or the exhaust pressure pipe is disconnected, damaged, misconnected or clogged.
  • Failed exhaust differential pressure sensor
  • Harness damage in exhaust differential pressure sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check exhaust pressure hose and exhaust pressure pipe
Check the lines between the exhaust differential pressure sensor and the exhaust centre pipe (DPF) (i.e, exhaust pressure hose and exhaust pressure pipe) for disconnection, damage, wrong connection or clogging.
Are the check results normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair or replace the exhaust pressure hose or exhaust pressure pipe.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 120: Exhaust differential pressure sensor
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between exhaust differential pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal DDP5
Check power supply line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between exhaust differential pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal DDPS
Check output line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between exhaust differential pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal DDPE
Check earthing line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Diagnosis code recheck after replace the exhaust differential pressure sensor
After replacing the exhaust differential pressure sensor, check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Replacing the exhaust differential pressure sensor.
(2) After replacing the exhaust differential pressure sensor, carry out the forcible DPF regeneration. [Refer to GROUP 17 - Emission Control <DIESEL>, Diesel Particulate Filter (DPF) System, Forcible DPF Regeneration <4N14> or <4N15>].
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(4) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(5) Drive the vehicle.
(6) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P2453 set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
The check is end.
