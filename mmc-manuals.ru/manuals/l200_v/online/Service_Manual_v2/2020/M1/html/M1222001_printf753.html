FORM-IN-PLACE GASKET (FIPG)
This transmission has several areas where the form-in-place gasket (FIPG) is used for sealing. To ensure that the FIPG fully serves its purpose, it is necessary to observe some precautions when applying it. Bead size, continuity and location are of paramount importance.
Too thin a bead could cause leaks. Too thick a bead, on the other hand, could be squeezed out of location, causing blocking or narrowing of fluid passages. To prevent leaks or blocking of passages, therefore, it is absolutely necessary to apply the FIPG evenly without a break, while observing the correct bead size.
FIPG hardens as it reacts with the moisture in the atmospheric air, and it is usually used for sealing metallic flange areas.
cautionWhen re-applying liquid gasket (FIPG), be sure that:
  1. Residues of FIPG are cleared from all the ins and outs of parts;
  2. Use parts cleaner (MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE Part No. MZ100387) or equivalent to well degrease the FIPG-applied surface.
  3. FIPG is correctly applied in accordance with FIPG Application.
 
 
Disassembly
Parts sealed with a FIPG can be easily removed without need for the use of a special method. In some cases, however, the FIPG in joints may have to be broken by tapping parts with a mallet or similar tool.
 
 
Surface Preparation
Thoroughly remove all substances deposited on the FIPG application surface, using a gasket scraper.
Make sure that the FIPG application surfaces is flat and smooth. Also make sure that the surface is free from oils, greases and foreign substances. Do not fail to remove old FIPG that may remain in the fastener fitting holes.
 
 
FIPG Application
Applied FIPG bead should be of the specified size and free of any break. FIPG can be wiped away unless it has completely hardened. Install the mating parts in position while the FIPG is still wet. Do not allow FIPG to spread beyond the sealing areas during installation. Avoid operating the transmission or letting oils or water come in contact with the sealed area before a time sufficient for FIPG to harden (approximately one hour) has passed.
FIPG application method may vary from location to location. Follow the instruction for each particular case described later in this manual.

DISASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
<<A>> SELF-LOCKING NUT REMOVAL
1. The hole on the gearshift shaft should be positioned as shown.
2. Engage the 3rd synchronizer sleeve into the 3rd position, and the 4th synchronizer sleeve into the 4th position, respectively.
3. Insert a tool into the hole on the gearshift shaft.
4. Turn the gearshift shaft approximately 7 degree angle.
5. Slide down the gearshift shaft to engage it into the 2nd position.
6. Make sure that the input shaft and the countershaft are counter-held.
7. Using the special tool lock nut wrench (46) (MD998810 or MD998835) to remove the self-locking nut.
 
 
 
<<B>> BEARING REMOVAL
Using the special tools to remove the bearing.
  • Bearing puller (MD998020)
  • Oil seal and bearing installer (MB991203)
 
 
<<C>> 3RD AND 4TH SYNCHRONIZER HUB AND 3RD SPEED GEAR REMOVAL
Using the special tools to remove the 3RD and 4TH synchronizer hub and 3RD speed gear.
  • Side bearing puller (MB990810)
  • Claw (MD999566)
 
 
 
REASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< 3RD AND 4TH SYNCHRONIZER HUB AND 3RD SPEED GEAR INSTALLATION
1. Using special tools to install the 3RD and 4TH synchronizer hub and 3RD speed gear.
  • Installer cap (MD998812)
  • Installer (100) (MD998813)
  • Installer adapter (40) (MD998819)
2. Press the input shaft with a press and install the 3RD and 4TH synchronizer hub and 3RD speed gear.
 
 
>>B<< BUSHING INSTALLATION
1. Use special tool installer bar (MB990938), install the bushing.
2. Press the gearshift 3RD and 4TH fork with a press and install the bushing.
 
 
>>C<< 3RD AND 4TH SYNCHRONIZER SLEEVE INSTALLATION
The identification groove on the 3rd and 4th synchronizer sleeves should face towards the clutch housing when the sleeves are inserted into the gearshift 3rd and 4th forks, respectively.
 

 
 
>>D<< SNAP RING INSTALLATION
1. Install the thickest snap ring that can be fitted in the snap ring groove of the input shaft.
2. Make sure that the 3RD and 4TH synchronizer sleeve end play meets the standard value.
Standard value: -0.01 to 0.09 mm
 
 
 
>>E<< BEARING INSTALLATION
1. Check the installation direction of the bearing, and put it on the countershaft.
2. Use special tools to install the bearing.
  • Installer cap (MD998812)
  • Installer (100) (MD998813)
  • Installer adapter (42) (MD998820)
3. Press the countershaft with a press and install the bearing.
 
 
 
>>F<< SELF-LOCKING NUT INSTALLATION
1. The gearshift 3rd and 4th rails should protrude by approximately 54.8 mm. When the rails are at this position, the transmission shift position is at neutral. If the protrusion amount deviates significantly from approximately 54.8 mm, adjust it by sliding up or down the respective rail(s).
2. The hole on the gearshift shaft should be positioned as shown.
3. Engage the 3rd synchronizer sleeve into the 3rd position, and the 4th synchronizer sleeve into the 4th position, respectively.
4. Insert a tool into the hole on the gearshift shaft.
5. Turn the gearshift shaft approximately 7 degree angle.
6. Slide down the gearshift shaft to engage it into the 2nd position.
7. Make sure that the input shaft and the countershaft are counter-held.
8. Using the special tool lock nut wrench (46) (MD998810 or MD998835) to install the self-locking nut.
9.
caution
  • Do not damage other parts.
  • Do not apply the excessive load to shaft.
Caulk into the overall countershaft groove.
10. Make sure of firmly caulking self-locking nut on the groove, after caulking.

DISASSEMBLY SERVICE POINT
 
 
<<A>> BALL BEARING REMOVAL
1. Use special tool remover (MD998801), remove the ball bearing.
2. Press the rear output shaft with a press and remove the ball bearing.
 
 

 
 
REASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< BALL BEARING INSTALLATION
1. Check the installation direction of the ball bearing, and put it on the rear output shaft.
2. Use special tools to install the ball bearing.
  • Installer (100) (MD998813)
  • Installer adapter (44) (MD998821)
3. Press the rear output shaft with a press and install the ball bearing.
 

 
 
>>B<< SYNCH SPRING/SYNCH KEY INSTALLATION
Install two synch springs. When installing the springs, Make sure that steps of front and rear spring s are positioned on a synch key, but not on the same key.
 

 
 
>>C<< CLUTCH SLEEVE INSTALLATION
Check that the clutch sleeve is in the correct direction for installation.
 

 
 
>>D<< CLUTCH HUB INSTALLATION
Check that the clutch hub is in the correct direction for installation.
 

 
 
>>E<< SNAP RING INSTALLATION
1. Install the thickest snap ring that can be fitted in the snap ring groove of the rear output shaft.
2. Make sure that the clutch hub axial play meets the standard value.
Standard value: 0.15 mm or less
