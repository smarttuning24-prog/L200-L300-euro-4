DISASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
<<A>> TIE ROD REMOVAL
1. Move the bellows and pull the rack bar toward arrow direction.
2.
cautionDo not tighten the vise strongly in order not to damage the rack bar.
Wrap rack bar with cloth in order not to damage, and fix the rack bar with a vise.
3. Use the tool to remove the tie rod.
 

 
 
<<B>> RACK SUPPORT COVER REMOVAL
Using special tool torque wrench socket (MB992351), remove the rack support cover from the steering gear.
 

 
 
<<C>> LOWER OIL SEAL/PINION AND VALVE ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
Using a plastic hammer, gently tap the pinion to remove it.
 

 
 
<<D>> SEAL RING REMOVAL
cautionWhen cutting the seal ring, be careful not to damage the pinion and valve assembly or the rack.
Cut the seal ring and remove it from the pinion and valve assembly or the rack.
 

 
 
<<E>> UPPER BEARING/UPPER OIL SEAL REMOVAL
Using a socket, remove the oil seal and the ball bearing from the valve housing simultaneously.
 

 
 
<<F>> CIRCLIP REMOVAL
cautionIf the rack stopper is first turned anti-clockwise, the circlip will get caught in the slot in the housing and the rack stopper will not turn.
1. Turn the rack stopper clockwise until the end of the circlip comes out of the slot in the rack housing.
2. Turn the rack stopper anti-clockwise to remove the circlip.
 

 
 
<<G>> RACK STOPPER/RACK BUSHING ASSEMBLY/O-RING/OIL SEAL/RACK ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
1. Pull out the rack slowly. Take out the rack stopper and the rack bushing at the same time.

2.
cautionDo not damage the oil seal press fitting surface.
Partially bend the oil seal and remove it from the rack bushing.
 

 
 
<<H>> LOWER BEARING REMOVAL
Use a brass bar or special tool brass bar (MB990939) to remove the ball bearing from the gear housing.
 

 
 
<<I>> NEEDLE BEARING REMOVAL
cautionDo not open special tool needle bearing puller (MB991120) excessively to prevent damaging housing interior.
Use special tool needle bearing puller (MB991120) to remove the needle bearing from the rack housing.
 

 
 
<<J>> OIL SEAL REMOVAL
cautionBe careful not to damage the inner surface of the rack cylinder of the gear housing.
Use a piece of pipe or similar tool to remove the oil seal from the gear housing.
 
 
 
 
REASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< OIL SEAL INSTALLATION
1. Wrap the rack assembly teeth face with plastic tape, and apply a coating of MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE PSF (Power Steering Fluid) or DEXRON III, DEXRON II.
2. Insert the oil seal in rack assembly, and move to the piston.
 

 
 
>>B<< NEEDLE BEARING/LOWER BEARING INSTALLATION
1. Apply MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE PSF (Power Steering Fluid) or DEXRON III, DEXRON II to housing, bearing and oil seal press fitting surface.
2.
cautionPress-fit straight. The valve housing is aluminium, and may become deformed if press-fit on an angle.
Press fit needle bearing with the following special tools.
  • Bar (snap-in type) (MB990938)
  • Installer (MB991146)
 

 
 
>>C<< RACK ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
1.
cautionDo not plug the vent hole in the rack with grease.
Removal the plastic tape, and apply a coating of multipurpose grease to the rack teeth face.
2. Apply a coating of repair kit grease to the outer surface of the seal ring and the O-ring.
3. Slowly insert the rack from power cylinder side.
 

 
 
>>D<< OIL SEAL/O-RING/RACK BUSHING ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
1. Apply MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE PSF (Power Steering Fluid) or DEXRON III, DEXRON II to the outer surface of the oil seal. Using special tool installer adapter (MB991118), press in the oil seal until it is flush with the bushing end face.
2. Apply MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE PSF (Power Steering Fluid) or DEXRON III, DEXRON II to the oil seal inner surface and the O-ring.
3. Wrap the rack end with plastic tape, and push the rack bushing onto the rack.
 

 
 
>>E<< CIRCLIP INSTALLATION
cautionInsert the circlip to the rack stopper hole while turning the rack stopper clockwise.
Insert the circlip to the rack stopper hole through cylinder hole. Turn the rack stopper clockwise and insert the circlip firmly.
 

 
 
>>F<< UPPER OIL SEAL/UPPER BEARING INSTALLATION
Apply a coating of MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE PSF (Power Steering Fluid) or DEXRON III, DEXRON II to the outside of the upper oil seal/upper bearing. Using the following special tools, press the upper oil seal/upper bearing into the valve housing.
  • MB990938: Bar (Snap-in type)
  • MB991203: Oil Seal and Bearing Installer
 

 
 
>>G<< SEAL RING INSTALLATION
Because the seal rings expand after installation, tighten after installing by using special tool seal ring installer (MB991317) to compress the rings, or press down by hand.
 

 
 
>>H<< LOWER OIL SEAL INSTALLATION
cautionTo eliminate a seal malfunction at the valve housing alignment surface, the upper surface of the oil seal should project outward approximately 1 mm from the housing edge surface.
Using special tool torque tube bearing installer (MB990941), press the oil seal into the valve housing.
 

 
 
>>I<< END PLUG INSTALLATION
1. Apply specified sealant to the threaded part of the end plug.
Specified sealant:
3M™ AAD Part No. 8663 or equivalent
2. Secure the threaded portion of the end plug at two places by using a punch.
 

 
 
>>J<< RACK SUPPORT COVER/LOCK NUT INSTALLATION
1. Position the rack at its centre.
2. Apply specified sealant to the threaded part of the rack support cover.
Specified sealant:
3M™ AAD Part No. 8663 or equivalent
3. Use special tool torque wrench socket (MB992351) to tighten the rack support cover.
4. Use the special tools to hold the rack support cover, and then tighten the lock nut.
Tightening torque: 59 ± 10 N·m
 

 
 
>>K<< TOTAL PINION TORQUE ADJUSTMENT
1.
caution
  • Be sure there is no ratcheting or catching when operating the rack towards the shaft.
  • Measure the total pinion torque through the whole stroke of the rack.
Using special tool preload socket (MB991006), rotate the pinion shaft at the rate of one rotation in four to six seconds to check the total pinion torque and the change in torque.
Standard value:
0.6 - 2.0 N·m (Torque fluctuation: 0.4 N·m or less)
2.
cautionWhen adjusting, set at the highest value of the standard value range.
noteIf the total pinion torque cannot be adjusted to the standard value within the specified return angle, check the rack support cover components and replace any parts if necessary.
If the total pinion torque or the change in torque is outside the standard value, move the rack support cover 0 - 30 degree angle, and adjust the pinion torque again.
 

 
 
>>L<< TIE ROD INSTALLATION
1.
cautionDo not tighten the vise strongly in order not to damage the rack bar.
Wrap rack bar with cloth in order not to damage, and fix the rack bar with a vise.
2. Use the tool to install the tie rod.
Tightening torque: 89 ± 10 N·m
 

 
 
>>M<< BELLOWS BAND INSTALLATION
1.
caution
  • Hold the rack housing, and use special tool bellows band clipping tool (MB992908) to crimp the bellows band securely.
  • Crimp the bellows band until special tool (MB992908) touches the stopper.
Use special tool (MB992908) to crimp the bellows band.
2. Check that crimped width (A) is within the standard value.
Standard value (A): 2.4 - 2.8 mm
