Code No. P2128: Accelerator Pedal Position Sensor (Sub) Circuit High Input
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
Refer to Code P2127: Accelerator Pedal Position Sensor (Sub) Circuit Low Input .
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The accelerator pedal position sensor converts the opening degree of the accelerator pedal into the voltage signal to input it to the engine ECU.
  • The engine ECU uses this signal to determine the amount of basic fuel injection.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • 2 seconds later after the ignition switch has been in "ON" position.
  • Battery positive voltage is 6 to 16 V.
  • Accelerator pedal position sensor (main) is normal.
  • Accelerator pedal position sensor (main) output voltage is 0.2 V or higher and 2.8 V or lower.
Judgement Criterion
  • Accelerator pedal position sensor (sub) output voltage is 2.4 V or more.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The amount of depression of the accelerator pedal is detected with using the accelerator pedal position sensor (main) signal, which is calculated by taking half of the normal value.
  • The AS&G control is prohibited.
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
  • The speed limiter control is prohibited.
  • The cruise control is prohibit.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed accelerator pedal position sensor
  • Open circuit or harness damage in accelerator pedal position sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 10: Accelerator pedal position sensor (sub)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Perform resistance measurement at accelerator pedal position sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between terminal engine-ECU APE line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between accelerator pedal position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal APE
Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 10: Accelerator pedal position sensor (sub)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
 
STEP 5. Diagnosis code recheck after replace the accelerator pedal assembly
After replacing the accelerator pedal assembly, check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Replacing the accelerator pedal assembly.
(2) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(4) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(5) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P2128 set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
The check is end.
