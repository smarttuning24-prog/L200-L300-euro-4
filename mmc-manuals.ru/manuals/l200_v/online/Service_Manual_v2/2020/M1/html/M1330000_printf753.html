33 -FRONT SUSPENSION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > FRONT WHEEL ALIGNMENT CHECK AND ADJUSTMENT
FRONT WHEEL ALIGNMENT CHECK AND ADJUSTMENT
cautionWhen the suspension is adjusted, the calibration of steering wheel sensor must be performed. (Refer to GROUP 35C - On-vehicle Service ).
 
 
Measure wheel alignment with alignment equipment on a level surface. The front suspension, steering system, wheels, and tyres should be serviced to normal condition before measuring wheel alignment.
 
 
TOE-IN
Standard value:
  • At the centre of tyre tread: 0 - 5 mm
  • Toe-angle (per wheel): 0°00' - 0°12'
1. Adjust the toe-in by undoing the clip and lock nut, and turning the left and right tie rod turnbuckles by the same amount (in opposite directions).
noteThe toe will move out as the left turnbuckle is turned toward the front of the vehicle and the right turnbuckle is turned toward the rear of the vehicle.
2. Install the clip and tighten the lock nut to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 93 ± 15 N·m
3. Confirm that the toe-in is at the standard value.
4. Use a turning radius gauge to check that the steering angle is at the standard value (Refer to GROUP 37 - On-vehicle Service, Steering Angle Check ).

33 -FRONT SUSPENSION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > LOWER ARM BALL JOINT AXIAL PLAY CHECK
LOWER ARM BALL JOINT AXIAL PLAY CHECK
1. Raise the vehicle.
2. Remove the stabilizer link from the lower arm.
3. Move the lower arm up and down with your hands to check for an excessive play in the axial direction of the ball joint. If there is an excessive play, replace the lower arm assembly (Refer to ).
 
33 -FRONT SUSPENSION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > LOWER ARM BALL JOINT DUST COVER CHECK
LOWER ARM BALL JOINT COVER CHECK
1. Press the lower arm ball joint cover with your finger to check that there are no cracks or damage in the lower arm ball joint cover.
2. If the lower arm ball joint cover is cracked or damaged, replace the lower arm assembly (Refer to ).
noteIf the lower arm ball joint cover is cracked or damaged, it is possible that there may also be damage to the ball joint.

REMOVAL SERVICE POINT
 
 
<<A>> SLOTTED NUT/UPPER ARM BALL JOINT AND KNUCKLE CONNECTION REMOVAL
caution
  • Do not remove the slotted nut from ball joint. Loosen it and use special tool to avoid possible damage to ball joint threads.
  • Hang special tool with a cord to prevent it from falling.
  • Because it is very dangerous when the ball joint comes off, raise the lower arm using a jack or similar device, making sure that the rebound stopper is free from any reaction force.
  • Hold the upper arm with a pipe to loosen the nut. If you fail to do this, the upper arm will jump out when its ball joint is separated from the knuckle.
1. Install special tool ball joint remover (MB992011) as shown in the figure.
2. Turn the bolt and knob as necessary to make the jaws of special tool parallel, tighten the bolt by hand and confirm that the jaws are still parallel.
noteWhen adjusting the jaws in parallel, make sure the knob is in the position shown in the figure.
3. Tighten the bolt with a wrench to disconnect the ball joint.

>>A<< REBOUND STOPPER INSTALLATION

UPPER ARM BALL JOINT STARTING TORQUE CHECK

1. After shaking the ball joint stud several times, install the nut to the stud and use special tool preload socket (MB990326) to measure the starting torque of the ball joint.
noteRotation starting torque measurement should be carried out with parts temperature above 0°C.
Standard value: 0.4 - 5.6 N·m
2. If the measured value exceeds the standard value, replace the upper arm assembly (Refer to ).
3. If the measured value is lower than the standard value, check that the upper arm ball joint turns smoothly without excessive play. If so, it is possible to use that ball joint.

2. If the dust cover is cracked or damaged, replace the upper arm assembly (Refer to ).
noteIf the dust cover is cracked or damaged, it is possible that there may also be damage to the ball joint.

DISASSEMBLY SERVICE POINTS
 
 
<<A>> SELF-LOCKING NUT REMOVAL
1.
cautionThe locking nut for the piston rod inside the shock absorber may be loose. Do not use an impact wrench to loosen the self-locking nut.
Install special tool spring compressor (MB991796) to special tool attachment B (MB991793) as shown in the figure.
2. Set the shock absorber assembly to the following special tools:
  • Spring compressor (MB991793)
  • Attachment B (MB991796)
  • Upper plate (MB991794)
  • Fixture (MB991830)
noteUse the bolts and nuts removed from the vehicle to secure the shock absorber assembly and tighten them lightly by hand.
3. After setting the shock absorber assembly, operate the spring compressor and compress the coil spring by approximately 5 mm.
4. Use the following special tools to secure the strut, and then remove the self-locking nut.
  • Wrench (MB991681)
  • Socket (MB992047)
 
 
 
<<B>> SHOCK ABSORBER DISPOSAL PROCEDURES
cautionWear the protective glasses. Although the gas is harmless, drilling chips may be blown out by the gas.
Before disposal of the shock absorber, place the shock absorber on the level surface with the piston rod extended, and make a hole of approximately 3 mm in diameter at the point shown in the figure to discharge the gas.

>>A<< COIL SPRING INSTALLATION

>>B<< SPRING BRACKET ASSEMBLY INSTALLATION
install the spring bracket assembly which faces the shock absorber as shown in the illustration.
 

 
 
>>C<< SELF-LOCKING NUT INSTALLATION
1. Set the shock absorber assembly to the following special tools:
  • Spring compressor (MB991793)
  • Attachment B (MB991796)
  • Upper plate (MB991794)
  • Fixture (MB991830)
noteUse the bolts and nuts removed from the vehicle to secure the shock absorber assembly and tighten them lightly by hand.
2.
cautionThe locking nut for the piston rod inside the shock absorber may be loose. Do not use an impact wrench to tighten the self-locking nut.
Using the following special tools tighten the self-locking nut.
  • Wrench (MB991681)
  • Socket (MB992047)
Tightening torque: 22 ± 2 N·m

STABILIZER LINK BALL JOINT TURNING TORQUE CHECK
1. After shaking the ball joint stud several times, install the nut to the stud and use special tool preload socket (MB990326) to measure the turning torque of the ball joint.
Standard value: 0.5 - 3.5 N·m
2. When the measured value exceeds the standard value, replace the stabilizer link (Refer to ).
3. When the measured value is lower than the standard value, check that the ball joint turns smoothly without excessive play. If so, it is possible to re-use that ball joint.

1. Check the dust cover for cracks or damage by pushing it with your finger.
2. If the dust cover is cracked or damaged, replace the stabilizer link (Refer to ).
noteCracks or damage of the dust cover may cause damage to the ball joint.
