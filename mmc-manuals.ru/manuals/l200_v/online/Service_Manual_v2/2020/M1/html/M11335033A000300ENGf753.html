Code No. P0016: Crank Angle Sensor/Camshaft Position Sensor Phase Problem
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • Refer to Code No. P0335: Crank Angle Sensor System <4N14> .
  • Refer to Code No. P0340: Camshaft Position Sensor System <4N14> .
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The engine-ECU monitors the signals input from the crank angle sensor and camshaft position sensor.
  • The engine-ECU compares these input pulse signals to check the position relations for validity.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V
  • One of the following two is true.
    1. 2 seconds have passed after the ignition switch is turned on, the crank angle sensor and camshaft position sensor output signals are normal, and the engine rotation speed is 1,200 r/min or lower.
    2. Cranking is in progress, the engine rotation speed is 350 r/min or faster, and the engine cooling water temperature is -30°C or higher.
Judgement Criterion
  • The phase shift angle between the crank and camshaft position sensor signals exceeds its threshold value.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The rail pressure is controlled in the range between 50 and 100 MPa.
  • The fuel injection amount is restricted (Output restriction).
  • The open angle of the accelerator pedal is controlled.
  • The throttle valve is opened (Electrical discontinuity).
  • The EGR control is stopped (Fully closed).
  • The DPF regeneration is prohibited.
  • The AS&G control is prohibited.
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
  • The speed limiter control is prohibited.
  • The cruise control is prohibit.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed crank angle sensor
  • Failed camshaft position sensor
  • Harness damage in crank angle sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Harness damage in camshaft position sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed timing chain
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the diagnosis code.
Is any other diagnosis code than No. P0016 set?
Inspection chart for diagnosis codes (Refer to ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check the timing chain
See the timing marks to check for skipped gear teeth.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Align the timing mark of the timing chain.
 
STEP 3. Check crank angle sensor and camshaft position sensor for correct mounting condition
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair.
 
STEP 4. Check harness between camshaft position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal 5V
Check power supply line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between camshaft position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal SGCE
Check earthing line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Check harness between camshaft position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal SGC
Check output line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 7. Check harness between crank angle sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal SGTB
Check power supply line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 8. Check harness between crank angle sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal SGTG
Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 9. Check harness between crank angle sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal SGT
Check output line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 10. Check timing mark on the timing chain
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Align the timing marks on timing chain.
 
STEP 11. Check camshaft sensing portion
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Replace the inlet camshaft.
 
STEP 12. Diagnosis code recheck after replace the crank angle sensor
After replacing the crank angle sensor, check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Replacing the common rail assembly and common tubes.
(2) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(4) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position and start the engine.
(5) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P0016 set?
Go to Step 13.
The check is end.
