Inspection Procedure 1: When the heated steering wheel switch is turned on, the heated steering wheel is not activated.
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If the heated steering wheel cannot be activated when the heated steering wheel switch is turned on with the heated steering wheel deactivated, a component of the heated steering wheel or the power supply lines to the heated steering wheel may be defective.
 
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the heated steering wheel switch
  • Malfunction of the clock spring
  • Malfunction of the heated steering wheel relay
  • Malfunction of the heated steering wheel-ECU
  • Malfunction of the steering wheel (incorporating heater element)
  • Malfunction of the harness wire(s) or connector(s)
    • Short or open circuit between the heated steering wheel-ECU and the fusible link or loose connector contact
    • Open circuit between the body earth and the heated steering wheel relay or loose connector contact
    • Open circuit between the body earth and the heated steering wheel-ECU or loose connector contact
 
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the heated steering wheel switch.
Check the heated steering wheel switch (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the heated steering wheel switch (Refer to ).
 
STEP 2. Check the clock spring.
Check whether the clock spring is in good condition (Refer to GROUP 52B - Air Bag Module and Clock Spring ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Replace the clock spring (Refer to GROUP 52B - Air Bag Module and Clock Spring ).
 
STEP 3. Check the heated steering wheel relay.
Check the heated steering wheel relay (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the heated steering wheel relay .
 
STEP 4. Resistance measurement at the heated steering wheel relay connector (earth terminal).
(1) Remove the heated steering wheel relay, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Resistance between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 1) and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. Check of open circuit in earth line between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No.1) and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 6. Voltage measurement at the heated steering wheel relay connector (IG terminal).
(1) Removal the heated steering wheel relay, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Tern the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Measure the voltage in IG line between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 2) and the body earth.
OK: Approximately system voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in IG1 line between the junction block and the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 2).
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 8. Voltage measurement at the heated steering wheel relay connector (power supply terminal).
(1) Remove the heated steering wheel relay, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Measure the voltage between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 4) and body earth.
OK: Approximately system voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Go to Step 9.
 
STEP 9. Check the fusible link and relay box.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair the fusible link and relay box.
 
STEP 10. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in power supply line between the fusible link No. 3 and the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 4) connector.
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 11. Resistance measurement at the heated steering wheel-ECU connector (HGND terminal).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Resistance between the heated steering wheel-ECU connector (HGND terminal) and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Go to Step 12.
 
STEP 12. Check of open circuit in earth line between the heated steering wheel-ECU connector (HGND terminal) and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 13. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in SHIG line between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 3) and the heated steering wheel-ECU connector (SHIG terminal).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 14. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in SHSW line between the heated steering wheel relay connector (terminal No. 3) and the heated steering wheel-ECU connector (SHSW terminal).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 15. Retest the system
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions).
Replace the heated steering wheel-ECU (Refer to ). Then go to Step 16.
 
STEP 16. Retest the system
Is the check result normal?
This diagnosis is complete.
Replace the steering wheel (Refer to ).
