21 -CLUTCH > 21A-CLUTCH > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > CLUTCH PEDAL CHECK AND ADJUSTMENT
CLUTCH PEDAL CHECK AND ADJUSTMENT
1. Turn back the carpet etc. under the clutch pedal.
2. Measure the clutch pedal clevis pin play (A). If the play is outside the standard value, go to Step 3.
Standard value (A): 1 - 3 mm
3. Remove the clutch switch (Refer to ).

4.
cautionDo not push in the master cylinder pushrod at this time, otherwise the clutch will not operate properly.
If the clutch pedal height is not within the standard value, loosen the setting nut of pushrod, then turn the pushrod to adjust the clutch pedal height to the standard value.
5. Check that the height (B) as well as the clearance (C) between the clutch pedal and stopper when the clutch is disengaged are within the standard value.
Standard value (B):
  • 117 - 122 mm <LHD>
  • 133.5 - 139 mm <RHD>
Standard value (C):
  • 30 mm or more <LHD>
  • 35 mm or more <RHD>
6. If the clutch pedal play and height as well as the clearance between the clutch pedal and stopper when the clutch is disengaged are not within the standard value, the air intrusion into the hydraulic system may have occurred, or either the clutch master cylinder or the clutch release cylinder may have an abnormality. In this case, perform air bleeding, or check the clutch master cylinder or the clutch release cylinder, and replace if necessary.
7. Install the clutch switch (Refer to ).
8. If the clutch pedal clevis pin play is not within the standard value, loosen the setting nut and move the pushrod to adjust.
9. After the adjustments, confirm that the clutch pedal free play (measured at the face of the pedal pad) and the distance between the clutch pedal (the face of the pedal pad) and the floorboard when the clutch is disengaged are within the standard value ranges.
Standard value (D): 4 - 13 mm
Standard value (C):
  • 30 mm or more <LHD>
  • 35 mm or more <RHD>
10. If the measured free play and distance do not agree with the standard value ranges, it is probably the result of either air in the hydraulic system or a faulty master cylinder or clutch. Bleed the air, or disassemble and inspect the master cylinder or clutch.
11. Reinstall the carpet, etc.
 
21 -CLUTCH > 21A-CLUTCH > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > CLUTCH BLEEDING
CLUTCH BLEEDING
cautionUse the specified brake fluid. Do not mix brake fluids.
Specified fluid: Brake fluid DOT 3 or DOT 4
1. Connect a hose with a bottle to the bleeder screw.
2. Open the bleed nipple.
3. Depress the clutch pedal slowly. Open the bleeder screw to let air and brake fluid out. Close the bleeder screw. Release the clutch pedal. Repeat until only brake fluid and no air comes out.
4. After air bleeding, tighten the bleeder screw.
5. Check that the brake fluid reservoir level stays between "MAX" and "MIN" marks throughout the clutch bleeding process.

CLUTCH INTERLOCK SWITCH CONTINUITY CHECK
cautionDo not apply grease or lubricant to the switch and the switch installation section to avoid malfunction of the switch. In addition, do not use gloves which have grease on them.
1. Check the clutch pedal.
2. Connect an ohmmeter to the clutch interlock switch connector, and then check for continuity when the clutch pedal is fully depressed and when it is released outward.
 
Tester connectionPedal positionSpecified condition
1-2Fully depressedContinuity (Less than 2 ohms)
ReleasedOpen circuit
3. If the continuity is incorrect, replace the clutch interlock switch (Refer to ).

INSTALLATION SERVICE POINT
 
 
>>A<< STOPPER INSTALLATION
1. Insert the stopper straight into the stud bolt of the body.
Shall not insert the dash pad between stopper and body panel.
2. Install the stopper securely by turning it clockwise until its underside contacts with the body panel.
3. After the installation, slightly pull the stopper by hand to check that it cannot be removed easily (appropriate holding power: 100N or more).
 
 
 
>>B<< CLEVIS PIN INSTALLATION
cautionDo not insert the clevis pin from the left to right direction. The snap pin may interfere with the clutch switch bracket and the snap pin may be broken.
Insert the clevis pin from the right to left direction.
