Code No. P0193: Rail Pressure Sensor Circuit High Input
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
Refer to Code No. P0191: Rail Pressure Sensor Range/Performance Problem .
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The rail pressure sensor converts the rail pressure into a voltage signal and inputs the signal to the engine-ECU.
  • In response to the signal, the engine-ECU corrects the fuel injection quantity.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V
  • 2 seconds later after the ignition switch has been in "ON" position or the engine has started up.
Judgement Criterion
  • The rail pressure sensor output voltage is 4.85 V or more.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The rail pressure is controlled in the range between 50 and 100 MPa.
  • The fuel injection amount is restricted (Output restriction).
  • The open angle of the accelerator pedal is restricted.
  • The throttle valve is closed (Fixed opening degree).
  • The EGR control is stopped (Fully closed).
  • The DPF regeneration is prohibited.
  • The AS&G control is prohibited.
  • The small injection quantity learning is prohibited.
  • The speed limiter control is prohibited.
  • The cruise control is prohibit.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed common rail assembly (integrated with rail pressure sensor)
  • Open circuit or harness damage in rail pressure sensor circuit or loose connector contact
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 63: Rail pressure sensor
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at rail pressure sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU FPS5 line and body earth.
OK: 4.9 to 5.1 V
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between rail pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal FPS5
Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 63: Rail pressure sensor
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
 
STEP 5. Perform resistance measurement at rail pressure sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Resistance between terminal engine-ECU FPSE line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Check harness between rail pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal FPSE
Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 7. Check harness between rail pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal FPS5
Check power supply line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 8. Check harness between rail pressure sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal FPS
Check output line for open circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 9. Voltage measurement at rail pressure sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at sensor side.
  • With the special tool test harness (MB991658) bridge only the mating terminals of engine-ECU FPS5 line and engine-ECU FPSE line of the disconnected connector.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU FPS line and body earth.
OK: 0.85 V or more
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Replace the common rail assembly (integrated with rail pressure sensor).
 
STEP 10. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 63: Rail pressure sensor
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
