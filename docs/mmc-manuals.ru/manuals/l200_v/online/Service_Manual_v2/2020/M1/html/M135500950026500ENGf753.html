NOTES WITH REGARD TO DIAGNOSIS
1. The Active Stability & Traction Control system (ASTC) is a system which controls the brake pressure and engine power by means of the operation of the ECU. Accordingly, the following symptoms may occur at times, but these are a sign of normal ASTC operation, and do not indicate a malfunction.
PhenomenonExplanation of phenomenon
When the engine starts, a knocking sound can be heard coming from the engine compartment.This sound occurs as a result of system operation checking, and is not a malfunction.
Although the accelerator pedal is depressed, the engine speed does not increase or drops.This occurs as a result of the Active Stability Control system (ASC) or Active Traction Control system (ATC) operation, and is not a malfunction.
  • Sound of the motor inside the hydraulic unit operation. (whine)
  • When ASTC operates, sound is generated from the vehicle chassis due to repeated brake application and release. (Thump: suspension; squeak: tyres)
This is the sound of normal system operation, and is not a malfunction.
Shocks are felt if the brake pedal is depressed when driving at low speed.This is due to system operation checking (starting-of check when the vehicle speed reaches a certain number of km/h) and is not a malfunction.
2. ASTC is system for assisting driver's operation. Accordingly, advise the customer to drive safely on such roads by lowering the vehicle speed and not being too overconfident.
3. Diagnosis detection condition can vary depending on the diagnosis code. Make sure that checking requirements listed in the "Comment" are satisfied when checking the trouble symptom again.
