ENGINE OILS
 
 
Health Warning
Prolonged and repeated contact with mineral oil will result in the removal of natural fats from the skin, leading to dryness, irritation and dermatitis. In addition, used engine oil contains potentially harmful contaminants which may cause skin cancer. Adequate means of skin protection and washing facilities must be provided.
 
 
Recommended Precautions
The most effective precaution is to adapt working practices which prevent, as far as practicable, the risk of skin contact with mineral oils, for example by using enclosed systems for handling used engine oil and by degreasing components, where practicable, before handling them. Other precautions:
  • Avoid prolonged and repeated contact with oils, particularly used engine oils.
  • Wear protective clothing, including impervious gloves where practicable.
  • Avoid contaminating clothes, particularly underpants, with oil.
  • Do not put oily rags in pockets, the use of overalls without pockets will avoid this.
  • Do not wear heavily soiled clothing and oil-impregnated foot-wear. Overalls must be cleaned regularly and kept separate from personal clothing.
  • Where there is a risk of eye contact, eye protection should be worn, for example, chemical goggles or face shields; in addition an eye wash facility should be provided.
  • Obtain first aid treatment immediately for open cuts and wounds.
  • Wash regularly with soap and water to ensure all oil is removed, especially before meals (skin cleansers and nail brushes will help). After cleaning, the application of preparations containing lanolin to replace the natural skin oils is advised.
  • Do not use petrol, kerosine, diesel fuel, gas oil, thinners or solvents for cleaning skin.
  • Use barrier creams, applying them before each work period, to help the removal of oil from the skin after work.
  • If skin disorders develop, obtain medical advice without delay.

12 -ENGINE LUBRICATION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > ENGINE OIL CHECK
ENGINE OIL CHECK
1.
cautionWhen burning away and removing soot accumulated in the diesel particulate filter (DPF), fuel can mix with the engine oil and an increase in the engine oil level may occur. This does not indicate an abnormality. However, if the engine oil level is at or over the "X" mark on the engine oil level gauge, change the engine oil.
Pull out the engine oil level gauge slowly and check that the engine oil level is in the illustrated range.
2. Check that the engine oil is not excessively dirty, that there is no engine coolant or fuel mixed in, and that it has sufficient viscosity.
 
12 -ENGINE LUBRICATION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > ENGINE OIL CHANGE
ENGINE OIL CHANGE
1. Start the engine and allow it to warm up until the temperature of the coolant reaches 80°C to 90°C.
2. Remove the oil filler cap.
3. Remove the under skid plate and engine room under cover front (Refer to GROUP 51 - Under Cover ).
4.
warningUse care as engine oil could be hot.
Remove the oil drain plug to drain engine oil.
5. Install a new oil drain plug gasket, and then tighten the drain plug to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 39 ± 5 N·m
6. Install the under skid plate and engine room under cover front (Refer to GROUP 51 - Under Cover ).
7. Refill with specified quantity of engine oil.
Specified Engine Oil
  • ACEA classification: "For service C1, C2, C3 or C4"
  • JASO classification: "For service DL-1"
Total quantity (Includes volume inside oil filter and oil cooler):
  • 6.6 L <4N14>
  • 8.4 L <4N15>
noteSelect engine oil of the proper SAE viscosity number according to the atmospheric temperature.
8. Install the oil filler cap.
9. Check oil level indicate below maximum marking.
10. Warm up engine for 10 minutes and wait for further 10 minutes.
11. Check that the oil level is between maximum marking and minimum marking.
12. If no, add the engine oil until the oil level gauge indicates between maximum marking and minimum marking.
 
12 -ENGINE LUBRICATION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > ENGINE OIL FILTER REPLACEMENT
OIL FILTER REPLACEMENT
1. Remove the oil drain plug to drain engine oil (Refer to ).
2. Use special tool oil filter wrench (MB991396) or commercially-available tool to remove the oil filter.
3. Clean the oil filter installing surface on the cylinder block side.
4. Apply a small amount of engine oil to the new oil filter gasket.
5. For the specified tightening torque, refer to the caution label attached on the oil filter.
6. Tighten the oil filter until the gasket contacts with the installation surface.
7. In the same manner as removal, use special tool oil filter wrench (MB991396) or commercially-available tool to tighten the oil filter to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: Approximately 1 turn (14 ± 2 N·m)
8. Install the oil drain plug and refill the engine oil (Refer to ).
9. Rev the engine a few times, and check to be sure that no engine oil leaks from the installation section of the oil filter.
 
12 -ENGINE LUBRICATION > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > OIL PRESSURE CHECK<4N14>
OIL PRESSURE CHECK <4N14>
1. Remove the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor (Refer to ).
2. Install the oil pressure gauge.
noteUse an adapter of M14 × 1.5 thread.
3. Run the engine to warm it.
4. After the engine has been warmed up, check that oil pressure is within the standard value.
Standard value:
  • At idle: 29 kPa or more
  • At 3,500 r/min: 294 to 686 kPa
5. Remove the oil pressure gauge.
6. Install the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor (Refer to ).

REMOVAL SERVICE POINT
 
 
<<A>> ENGINE OIL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE SENSOR/CONNECTOR REMOVAL
cautionBe careful not to damage the connector portion (made of resin) of the sensor with the tool.
Use special tool oil pressure switch wrench (MB992118) to remove the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor.
If the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor and connector come off together, remove the connector from the pressure and temperature sensor.
 
 
 
 
INSTALLATION SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< CONNECTOR INSTALLATION
1.
cautionTo install the connector, be careful to keep foreign material from entering oil channels.
Wipe off the dirt on the connector and connector mounting surface.
2. Tighten the connector to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 34 ± 6 N·m
 
 
 
>>B<< O-RING/ENGINE OIL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE SENSOR INSTALLATION
1.
caution
  • Do not reuse the O-ring.
  • Install the O-ring after wrap a tape not having adhesion (such as a seal tape) round the notch on the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor to prevent it from damage. The damaged O-ring may cause oil leakage.
Install the O-ring to the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor.
2.
cautionTo install the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor, be careful to keep foreign material from entering oil channels.
Wipe off the dirt on the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor and engine oil pressure and temperature sensor mounting surface.
3. Apply a little amount of engine oil to the O-ring.
4. Install the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor to the connector.
5. In the same manner as removal, use special tool oil pressure switch wrench (MB992118) to tighten the engine oil pressure and temperature sensor to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 20 ± 4 N·m
 
 
12 -ENGINE LUBRICATION > ENGINE OIL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE SENSOR<4N14> > ENGINE OIL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE SENSOR INSPECTION
INSPECTION
 
 
ENGINE OIL PRESSURE AND TEMPERATURE SENSOR CHECK
1. With temperature sensing portion of engine oil pressure and temperature sensor immersed in hot engine oil, check the resistance between terminal No. 3 and No.4.
Standard value:
  • 91.3 to 106.0 Ω (at -20°C)
  • 30.7 to 34.9 Ω (at 0°C)
  • 11.7 to 13.2 Ω (at 20°C)
  • 5.0 to 5.6 Ω (at 40°C)
  • 2.3 to 2.6 Ω (at 60°C)
  • 1.2 to 1.3 Ω (at 80°C)
  • 639 to 720 Ω (at 100°C)
  • 366 to 414 Ω (at 120°C)
  • 216 to 254 Ω (at 140°C)

REMOVAL SERVICE POINT
 
 
<<A>> ENGINE OIL PRESSURE SWITCH/CONNECTOR REMOVAL
caution
  • The sealant is applied to the engine oil pressure switch. Remove the engine oil pressure switch with care not to break it.
  • Be careful not to damage the connector portion (made of resin) of the switch with the tool.
Use special tool oil pressure switch wrench (MB992118) to remove the engine oil pressure switch.
If the engine oil pressure switch and connector come off together, remove the connector from the engine oil pressure switch.
 
 
 
 
INSTALLATION SERVICE POINTS
 
 
>>A<< CONNECTOR INSTALLATION
1.
cautionTo install the connector, be careful to keep foreign material from entering oil channels.
Wipe off the dirt and sealant on the connector and connector mounting surface.
2. Tighten the connector to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 19 ± 3 N·m
 
 
 
>>B<< ENGINE OIL PRESSURE SWITCH
1.
cautionTo install the engine oil pressure switch, be careful to keep foreign material from entering oil channels.
Wipe off the dirt and sealant on the engine oil pressure switch and engine oil pressure switch mounting surface.
2.
cautionUse care not to allow the sealant to plug the oil passage.
Apply sealant to the engine oil pressure switch thread.
Specified sealant: MITSUBISHI MOTORS GENUINE Part No. MZ100077 or equivalent
noteInstall the engine oil pressure switch immediately after applying sealant.
3. Install the engine oil pressure switch to the connector.
4.
cautionAfter the installation, until a sufficient period of time (1 hour or more) elapses, do not apply the oil or water to the sealant application area or start the engine.
In the same manner as removal, use special tool oil pressure switch wrench (MB992118) to tighten the engine oil pressure switch to the specified torque.
Tightening torque: 19 ± 3 N·m
