Code No. P05EA: Engine Oil Bypass Valve Control Circuit Abnormal <4N14>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • Power is supplied to the oil relief control valve connector terminal from the engine control relay connector terminal.
  • The engine-ECU connector terminal OCV2 makes the power transistor in the unit be in "ON" position, and that makes currents go on the oil relief control valve connector terminal.
 
 
FUNCTION
The oil relief control valve changes the valve opening pressure of the oil relief valve according to the signals from the engine-ECU, and decreases the oil pressure applied to the engine.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position
  • During the oil relief control valve operation*.
note*: The operating conditions of the il relief control valve are as follows:
  • Engine speed is 1,000 r/min or more and 3,000 r/min or less.
  • Engine load is 100 N·m or more and 200 N·m or less.
  • The small injection quantity learning is not in progress.
Judgement Criterion
  • A malfunction is detected in the oil relief control valve circuit.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
  • The fuel injection amount is restricted (Output restriction).
  • The open angle of the accelerator pedal is restricted.
  • The speed limiter control is prohibited.
  • The cruise control is prohibit.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed oil cooler bracket (integrated with oil relief control valve).
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in oil relief control valve circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed engine-ECU
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Perform resistance measurement at oil relief control valve connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at oil relief control valve side.
  • Resistance between terminals.
OK: 6.7 to 7.7 Ω (at 20°C)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the oil cooler bracket (integrated with oil relief control valve).
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at oil relief control valve connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal power supply line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair harness between engine control relay connector terminal and oil relief control valve connector terminal.
    • Check power supply line open/short circuit.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between oil relief control valve connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal OCV2
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position and during the oil relief control valve operation.
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P05EA set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
