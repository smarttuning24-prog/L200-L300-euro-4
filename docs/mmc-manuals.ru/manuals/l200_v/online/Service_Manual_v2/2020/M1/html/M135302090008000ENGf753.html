Code No. C1346: Abnormal Stop Lamp Circuit
 
 
caution
  • If there is any problem in the CAN bus lines, an incorrect diagnosis code may be set. Prior to this diagnosis, diagnose the CAN bus lines (Refer to GROUP 54C - CAN Bus Diagnostics Table ).
  • Whenever ECU is replaced, ensure that the CAN bus lines are normal.
  • When the hydraulic unit (integrated with ASTC-ECU) is replaced, always carry out the calibration of the steering wheel sensor and the G and yaw rate sensor (Refer to ).
 
 
OPERATION
  • ASTC-ECU monitors whether the stop lamp is ON or OFF.
  • The ASTC-ECU activates the stop lamp relay to illuminate the stop lamps.
  • The ASTC-ECU monitors the operating conditions of the stop lamp switch, the stop lamps and the stop lamp relay.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS
  • The system cannot recognise the operating condition of the stop lamps.
  • When the stop lamp relay is OFF, the ON/OFF status of the stop lamp switch does not coincide with the actual stop lamp illumination for at least one second.
  • Although the stop lamp relay is ON, the stop lamps remain off (do not come on) for at least one second.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
Current trouble
  • The stop lamp switch improperly installed
  • Malfunction of the stop lamp switch
  • Malfunction of the stop lamp relay
  • Damaged wiring harness or connector
  • Malfunction of the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU)
Past trouble
  • For diagnosis procedures, refer to How to treat past trouble (GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points ).
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III CAN bus diagnosis
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use the M.U.T.-III to diagnose the CAN bus lines.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair the CAN bus lines (Refer to GROUP 54C - CAN Bus Diagnostics Table ). Then go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Diagnosis code recheck after repairing CAN bus lines
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(4)
cautionDo not depress the brake pedal while the actuator test is in progress.
Use the M.U.T.-III to execute actuator test item No.11 "Stop lamp test mode".
(5) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. C1346 set?
Go to Step 3.
This diagnosis is complete.
 
STEP 3. Check the stop lamp operation
Check the stop lamp operation when the brake pedal is depressed. Check that the stop lamp and high-mount stop lamp illuminates when the brake pedal is depressed and that it goes out when the brake pedal is released.
OK:
  • When the brake pedal is released: Not illuminate
  • When the brake pedal is depressed: Illuminate
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the stop lamp system (Refer to GROUP 54A - Rear combination Lamp, Troubleshooting, Symptom Procedures, Inspection Procedure 4 ). Then go to Step 11.
 
STEP 4. M.U.T.-III other system data list
Check the data lists of the ETACS-ECU (Refer to GROUP 54A - ETACS, Troubleshooting, Data List Reference Table ).
  • Item No. 108: Stop lamp switch
OK:
  • ON (Brake pedal: Depressed)
  • OFF (Brake pedal: Released)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Perform the ETACS system input signal procedure (Refer to GROUP 54A - ETACS, Troubleshooting, Inspection procedure No. 10: The stop lamp switch signal not received ). Then go to Step 11.
 
STEP 5. M.U.T.-III actuator test
Use the M.U.T.-III to execute the actuator test below to check the stop lamps for proper operation (Refer to ).
  • Item No.11: Stop lamp test mode
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Measure the voltage at the ASTC-ECU connector (ASTP terminal)
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Check the voltage between the ASTC-ECU connector ASTP terminal and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in ASTP line between ASTC-ECU connector and stop lamp relay connector. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in power supply line between fusible link (3) and stop lamp relay connector
(1) Disconnect the ASTC-ECU connector and stop lamp relay connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Measure the resistance between the ASTC-ECU connector ASTP terminal and the stop lamp relay connector terminal No.7.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
(3) Measure the resistance between the ASTC-ECU connector ASTP terminal and body earth.
OK: No continuity (1 kΩ or more)
(4) Check the voltage between the stop lamp relay connector terminal No. 4 and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness. Then go to Step 11.
 
STEP 8. Check of open circuit in earth line between the stop lamp relay connector and body earth
(1) Disconnect the stop lamp relay connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Measure the resistance between the stop lamp relay connector terminal No.6 and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Replace the stop lamp relay (Refer to GROUP 35A - On-vehicle Service, Stop Lamp Relay Replacement). Then go to Step 11.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness. Then go to Step 11.
 
STEP 9. Check of open circuit in STP line between the ASTC-ECU connector and the stop lamp relay connector
(1) Disconnect the ASTC-ECU connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Depress the brake pedal.
(3) Check the voltage between the ASTC-ECU connector STP terminal and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness. Then go to Step 11.
 
STEP 10. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(4)
cautionDo not depress the brake pedal while the actuator test is in progress.
Use the M.U.T.-III to execute actuator test item No.11 "Stop lamp test mode".
(5) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. C1346 set?
Replace the hydraulic unit assembly (integrated with ASTC-ECU) (Refer to ). Then go to Step 11.
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
 
STEP 11. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(4)
cautionDo not depress the brake pedal while the actuator test is in progress.
Use the M.U.T.-III to execute actuator test item No.11 "Stop lamp test mode".
(5) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. C1346 set?
Return to Step 1.
The procedure is complete.
