TIME LAG TEST
2020 / L200/L200 SPORTERO / EU - Workshop Manual
23 -AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION > 23C-AUTOMATIC TRANSMISSION<R6AWH, V6AWH> > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > TIME LAG TEST
 
 
The time lag test indicates the measurement of time until a shock is felt after a shift change is performed during idle from "N" position to "D" or "R" position. This test checks the statuses of clutch, brake, and hydraulic pressure.
1.
warningFor safety, operators must not stand in front of and at the rear of the vehicle during this test.
Check the A/T fluid temperature.
  • A/T fluid temperature: 50 - 80°C
  • Switches for air conditioner, lamps, and others shall be OFF.
noteUse the M.U.T.-III to measure the A/T fluid temperature.
2. Raise the vehicle.
3. Pull the parking brake lever, and then fully depress the brake pedal.
4. Start the engine.
5.
caution
  • Perform the measurement for 3 times or more, and obtain the average value of the measurements.
  • The interval of time lag test must be 1 minute or more. (To eliminate the residual pressure of clutches and brakes)
Measure the time starting from the moment a shifting is performed from "N" to "D" or "R" position until a shock is felt.
Standard value:
"N" to "D" position: 1.2 seconds or less
"N" to "R" position: 1.2 seconds or less
 
 
 
TIME LAG TEST JUDGEMENT
Time lag test resultProbable cause
Time lag at shifting from "N" to "D" position is longer than the standard value.
  • Malfunction of the valve body (SL1 oil pressure system)
  • Malfunction of the clutch No. 1 (C1) (slip)
  • Malfunction of the one-way clutch No. 3 (F3)
  • Malfunction of the oil pump
  • Oil strainer clogged
Time lag at shifting from "N" to "R" position is longer than the standard value.
  • Malfunction of the valve body (SL4 oil pressure system)
  • Malfunction of the brake No. 1 (B1) (slip)
  • Malfunction of the brake No. 4 (B4) (slip)
  • Malfunction of the oil pump
  • Oil strainer clogged
