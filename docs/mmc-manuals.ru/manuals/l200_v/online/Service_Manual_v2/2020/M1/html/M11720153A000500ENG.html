Code No. P1564: Abnormal Voltage at Cruise Control Switches <4N15>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
This circuit judges the signals of each switches (speed limiter ON/OFF, cruise control ON/OFF, CANCEL, SET - and RES +) of the cruise control switch. The engine-ECU detects the state of the cruise control switches by sensing the voltages shown below.
  • When all switches are released: 4.7 to 5.0 V
  • When the cruise control ON/OFF switch is pressed: 0.9 to 1.7 V
  • When the CANCEL switch is pressed: 1.9 to 2.7 V
  • When the SET - switch is pressed: 2.9 to 3.6 V
  • When the RES + switch is pressed: 3.8 to 4.5 V
 
 
DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS
  • If the cruise control switches is operated, this diagnosis code will be set when the engine-ECU terminal voltage is different from the standard value.
  • Or, this code is set when the SET - switch or RES + switch is stuck to ON.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Damaged harness or connector.
  • Malfunction of the cruise control switch.
  • Malfunction of the clock spring.
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 136: Cruise control ON/OFF switch.
  • Item No. 137: Cruise cancel switch.
  • Item No. 138: Cruise set switch.
  • Item No. 139: Cruise resume switch.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at cruise control switch connector
(1) Remove the cruise control switch from the steering wheel with the cruise control switch connector connected (Refer to ).
(2) Connect the negative battery terminal that was disconnected when the driver's air bag module was removed.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(4) Do not operate the cruise control switch.
(5) Measure the terminal voltage between cruise control switch connector terminal engine-ECU CNTS line and body earth with cruise control switch connector connected.
OK: 4.7 to 5.0 V
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Perform voltage measurement at engine-ECU connector
(1) Remove the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU ).
(2) Connect special tool power plant ECU check harness (MB992291) between the engine-ECU and the body-side harness connector.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(4) Do not operate the cruise control switch.
(5) Measure the terminal voltage between special tool 58-pin connector terminal No. 139 (engine-ECU connector terminal CNTS) and body earth.
OK: 4.7 to 5.0 V
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 4.
 
STEP 4. Check the harness wire for short circuit to earth between engine-ECU connector terminal CNTS and the cruise control switch connector terminal
(1) Check the engine-ECU connector.
(2) Disconnect engine-ECU connector and measure at the harness connector side.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(4) Measure the continuity between engine-ECU connector terminal CNTS and body earth.
OK: No continuity
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 18.
Go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. Check the harness wire between engine-ECU connector terminal CNTS and clock spring connector terminal, and between clock spring connector terminal and cruise control switch connector terminal
Check power supply line for short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

  • Check clock spring itself (Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s Air Bag Module and Clock Spring Inspection ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.

STEP 19. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position and start the engine.
(4) Press the cruise control ON/OFF switch to turn the cruise control system to ON (the cruise control indicator lamp is turned on).
(5) After turning the cruise control system to ON, when 2 minutes or more has elapsed without operating the cruise control switches, use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P1564 set?
Replace the engine-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU ). Then go to Step 20.
It can be assumed that this malfunction is intermittent (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
