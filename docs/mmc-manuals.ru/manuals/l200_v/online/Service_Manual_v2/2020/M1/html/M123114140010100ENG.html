Inspection Procedure 20: Shock is significant at shifting from "N" to "D" range
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If a strong shock occurs when the selector lever is moved from the "N" to "D" position, the cause is probably an immixing of A/T fluid other than the specified one, a malfunction of the clutch control solenoid (SL1) system, mounting (engine mounting and transmission mounting insulator), input shaft speed sensor system, output shaft speed sensor system, inhibitor switch system, A/T fluid temperature sensor 1 system, or valve body assembly.
noteAs a cause of shock, the battery voltage abnormality, engine system malfunction, driveshaft malfunction (damage, wear, looseness, large deflection), unbalanced tyres, interference between drive line and body, or suspension malfunction (looseness) is suspected. However, in this item, it shall be presupposed that there is no abnormality to the above.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Malfunction of the clutch control solenoid (SL1) system
  • Malfunction of the mounting (engine mounting and transmission mounting insulator)
  • Malfunction of the input shaft speed sensor system
  • Malfunction of the output shaft speed sensor system
  • Malfunction of the inhibitor switch system
  • Malfunction of the A/T fluid temperature sensor 1 system
  • Immixing of A/T fluid other than the specified one
  • Malfunction of the valve body assembly [clutch control solenoid (SL1)]
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU
  • Malfunction of the transmission assembly [clutch No. 1 (C1), one-way clutch No. 3 (F3), etc.]

If the trouble symptom is not solved, replace the A/T-ECU. Then, check the trouble symptom again.

Check the clutches and brakes for damage, and replace if necessary. [It is highly possible that the clutch No. 1 (C1) or one-way clutch No. 3 (F3) that engage in 1st gear is damaged.]
The procedure is complete.
