Inspection Procedure 5: Although any vehicle is present within a detection area, the blind spot warning lamp on door mirror does not illuminate. (the system is on)
 
 
cautionBefore replacing the ECU, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
  • The BSW-ECU (master) controls the blind spot warning lamp (LH), the BSW-ECU (slave) controls the blind spot warning lamp (RH), respectively.
  • "BSW RADAR BLOCKED" may be displayed on the combination meter while the vehicle is driven. Refer to .
noteThe blind spot warning/rear cross traffic alert is affected by ambient environments and vehicle conditions. The customer may misunderstand the radar operation in the BSW-ECU, thus causing a complaint. Prior this diagnosis, determines whether the malfunction is cased by ambient environments or vehicle conditions.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Bad weather (heavy rain, snow, icing, etc.)
  • Road condition or ambient environment
  • Rear bumper contaminated or foreign material adhered
  • Improperly installed BSW-ECU (master/slave)
  • Malfunction of the BSW-ECU (master/slave)
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the rear bumper, BSW-ECU and their surrounding for contamination or foreign material.
Visually check the rear bumper, BSW-ECU and their surrounding for contamination or foreign material.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Remove and clean the blockage or foreign material.
 
STEP 2. Check the rear bumper for scratches or dents.
Visually check the rear bumper for scratches or dents.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Go to Step 3.
Replace the Rear bumper assembly (Refer to GROUP 51 - Rear Bumper Assembly ).
 
STEP 3. Check whether the BSW-ECUs (master/slave) are installed in right angle.
Refer to .
OK: -3° to +3°
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the bracket of the BSW-ECU, which installation angle is not within the permissible angle above (Refer to ).
 
STEP 4. Retest the system.
Drive the vehicle for approximately 10 minutes. Then check that the BSW works normally.
Is the check result normal?
Explain the customer that this system does not work under certain road conditions or ambient environments.
Replace the BSW-ECU, which corresponds to a defective blind spot warning lamp (Refer to ).
