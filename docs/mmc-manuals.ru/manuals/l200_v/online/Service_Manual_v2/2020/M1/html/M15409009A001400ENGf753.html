Inspection Procedure 2: Rear-view camera image is not correctly displayed. <Vehicles with MMCS>
 
 
cautionBefore replacing the rear-view camera or multivision display, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit, and the communication circuit are normal (Check that the voltage is 10 V or more).
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the screen of rear-view camera is not shown even if the shift lever <M/T> or selector lever <A/T> is "R" (Reverse) position, the rear-view camera, multivision display, the wiring harness, connectors, back-up lamp switch <M/T>, inhibitor switch <A/T> may be defective.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the rear-view camera
  • Malfunction of the multivision display
  • Malfunction of the back-up lamp switch <M/T>
  • Malfunction of the inhibitor switch <A/T>
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the multivision display operation.
Check if the multivision display normally and the sound is output.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Troubleshoot the MMCS (Refer to ).
 
STEP 2. Confirmation the service mode.
  1. Display the "Vehicle Signal Check" screen (Refer to ).
  2. Move the shift lever <M/T> or selector lever <A/T> to the "R" (reverse) position.
  3. Check that "Shift Position R" is "ON."
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check the back-up lamp switch <M/T> or inhibitor switch <A/T>.
Check the continuity of the back-up lamp switch <M/T> or inhibitor switch <A/T>. (Refer to GROUP 22C - Transmission , GROUP 23C - On-vehicle service, Inhibitor switch continuity check )
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the back-up lamp switch <M/T> or inhibitor switch <A/T>.
 
STEP 4. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in REV line between multivision display connector and back-up lamp switch <M/T> or inhibitor switch <A/T> connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 5. Confirmation the service mode.
  1. Display the "Communications & Connection Check" screen (Refer to ).
  2. Check that "Rear Camera" is "OK."
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Voltage measurement at multivision display connector (+B2 terminal).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Check the voltage between the multivision display connector (+B2 terminal) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair or replace the connector, or check and the repair wiring harness between fusible link and multivision display connector terminal +B2.
 
STEP 7. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in GND2 line between multivision display connector and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 8. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in CACC, CGND, CDET, CMP- and CMP+ line between multivision display connector and rear-view camera connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Trouble symptom recheck.
Check that the display of rear-view camera is shown normally.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the rear-view camera temporarily and go to Step 10.
 
STEP 10. Trouble symptom recheck.
Check that the display of rear-view camera is shown normally.
Is the check result normal?
Replace the rear-view camera.
Replace the multivision connector.
