Inspection Procedure 4: Starter Impossible (Starter not Operative) <4N15-A/T>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The power source is supplied from the battery to the starter connector terminal.
  • The battery voltage is applied to the starter relay connector terminal.
  • When the ignition switch "ST" signal is input to the engine-ECU connector terminal ST, the engine-ECU connector terminal STRL turns ON the power transistor inside the unit, and makes the current flow into the starter relay connector terminal to turn ON the relay.
  • When the starter relay is turned ON, the current flows into the starter connector terminal from the starter relay connector terminal to drive the starter motor.
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
The failure could possibly be caused by the malfunction of the starter or starter system circuits.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed battery.
  • Failed ignition switch. <Vehicles without KOS>
  • Failed KOS&OSS-ECU. <Vehicles with KOS>
  • Failed starter relay.
  • Failed starter motor.
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in starter system circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed inhibitor switch.
  • Failed engine-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check battery
Check battery (Refer to GROUP 54A - Battery, On-vehicle Service, Battery Test ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Replace the battery.
 
STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 40: Starter switch
OK:
  • ON (ignition switch: "ST" position)
  • OFF (ignition switch: "ON" position)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Perform voltage measurement at engine-ECU connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ST" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU ST line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
NO <Vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 4.
NO <Vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 4. Check ignition switch itself <Vehicles without KOS>
  • Ignition switch connector check.
  • Check ignition switch itself (Refer to GROUP 54A - Ignition Switch, Ignition Switch, Ignition Switch Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the ignition switch.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between ignition switch connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal ST <Vehicles without KOS>
Check power supply line for open/short circuit or damage.
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Check harness between KOS&OSS-ECU connector terminal STO and engine-ECU connector terminal ST <Vehicles with KOS>
Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Perform troubleshooting of OSS System (Refer to GROUP 42B - Troubleshooting, Trouble Symptom Chart ).
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 7. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 40: Starter switch
OK:
  • ON (ignition switch: "ST" position)
  • OFF (ignition switch: "ON" position)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
 
STEP 8. Check starter relay itself
  • Starter relay connector check.
  • Check starter relay itself (Refer to GROUP 16 - Starting System, On-vehicle Service, Starter Relay Continuity Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the starter relay.
 
STEP 9. Perform resistance measurement at starter relay connector
  • Remove the starter relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Resistance between terminal starter relay earthing line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair harness between starter relay connector terminal and body earth.
    • Check earthing line for open circuit and damage.
 
STEP 10. Check harness between engine-ECU connector terminal NTSW and starter relay connector terminal
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 11. Perform voltage measurement at starter relay connector
  • Remove the starter relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Transmission: "P" or "N" range
  • Ignition switch: "ST" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU STRL line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
NO : Go to Step 12.
 
STEP 12. Check inhibitor switch itself
  • Inhibitor switch connector check.
  • Check inhibitor switch itself (Refer to GROUP 23C - On-vehicle Service, Essential Service, Inhibitor Switch Continuity Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Repair or replace the connector, or replace the inhibitor switch.
 
STEP 13. Check harness between inhibitor switch connector terminal and starter relay connector terminal
Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 14. Check harness between engine-ECU connector terminal STRL and inhibitor switch connector terminal
Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 15. M.U.T.-III data list
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data list (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 413: Starter relay
OK:
  • ON (ignition switch: "ST" position)
  • OFF (ignition switch: "ON" position)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the engine-ECU.
 
STEP 16. Perform voltage measurement at starter relay connector
  • Remove the starter relay, and measure at relay box side.
  • Voltage between terminal starter relay power supply line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 17.
  • Repair or replace the connector, or check and repair harness between battery and starter relay connector terminal.
    • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
 
STEP 17. Perform voltage measurement at starter connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Transmission: "P" or "N" range
  • Ignition switch: "ST" position
  • Voltage between terminal starter relay output line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Go to Step 18.
 
STEP 18. Check harness between starter relay connector terminal and starter connector terminal
Check output line for open/short circuit.
Is the check result normal?
YES : Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Repair or replace the connector, or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 19. Perform voltage measurement at starter terminal
  • Disconnect terminal, and measure at harness side.
  • Voltage between terminal starter power supply line and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 20.
  • Repair or replace the terminal, or check and repair harness between battery and starter terminal.
    • Check power supply line for open/short circuit.
 
STEP 20. Check the trouble symptom
Does the trouble symptom persist?
Replace the starter.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
