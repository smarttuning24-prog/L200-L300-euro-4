15 -INTAKE AND EXHAUST > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > TURBOCHARGER BOOST PRESSURE CHECK
TURBOCHARGER SUPERCHARGING PRESSURE CHECK
1.
cautionConduct the driving test in a location where driving at full acceleration can be done with safety. 2 persons should be in the vehicle when the test is conducted; the person in the passenger seat should read the indications shown by the M.U.T.-III.
Set the vehicle to the pre-inspection condition.
2. Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position and then connect the M.U.T.-III to the diagnosis connector (Refer to GROUP 00 - Precautions Before Service, M.U.T.-III ).
3. Use the DIESEL system data list function "Item No. 6: boost pressure sensor" of the M.U.T.-III to check the supercharging pressure when the engine speed increases to approximately 3,000 r/min or more by driving at full acceleration in 2nd.
Standard value:
  • 225 to 270 kPa <4N14>
  • 235 to 275 kPa <(Except MIVEC)>
  • 240 to 280 kPa <4N15 (MIVEC)>
4. If the supercharging pressure deviates from the standard value, check the following items for possible cause.
  • Malfunction of the variable geometry actuator.
  • Malfunction of the variable geometry control solenoid valve.
  • Malfunction of the variable nozzle.
  • Malfunction of the manifold absolute pressure sensor.
  • Leakage of supercharging pressure.
  • Malfunction of the turbocharger.
  • Inadequate vacuum pressure to the variable geometry control solenoid valve.
  • Malfunction of the position sensor on variable geometry actuator.
5. When the indicated supercharging is more than standard value, supercharging control may be faulty, therefore check the followings.
  • Malfunction of the variable geometry actuator.
  • Malfunction of the variable geometry control solenoid valve.
  • Malfunction of the variable nozzle.
  • Malfunction of the manifold absolute pressure sensor.
  • Malfunction of the position sensor on variable geometry actuator.
 
15 -INTAKE AND EXHAUST > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > VARIABLE GEOMETRY ACTUATOR CHECK
VARIABLE GEOMETRY ACTUATOR CHECK
1. Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position and then connect the M.U.T.-III to the diagnosis connector (Refer to GROUP 00 - Precautions Before Service, M.U.T.-III ).
2. Remove the vacuum hose from the variable geometry actuator. Connect the hand vacuum pump to the nipple.
3.
cautionIn order to avoid damage to the diaphragm, do not apply a vacuum of -60 kPa or higher.
Apply the vacuum pressure (of approximately -57 kPa) until the variable geometry actuator link touches the stopper bolt. This allows the actuator rod to fully shrink.
Use the M.U.T.-III to see the diesel fuel system data list "Item No. 559: VGT position sensor" to check the characteristics of variable geometry position sensor.
Standard value: Maximum 1.2 mm
4. Leak the vacuum pressure gradually as much as possible to check the vacuum pressure when the variable geometry actuator rod moves approximately 1 mm in the extension direction.
Standard value: -46.7 to -54.7 kPa
5. If the vacuum pressure or variable geometry position sensor output significantly deviate from the standard value, check the variable geometry actuator, the variable nozzle or variable geometry position sensor connector. Replace the turbocharger assembly if necessary.
 
15 -INTAKE AND EXHAUST > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > VARIABLE GEOMETRY SOLENOID VALVE CHECK
VARIABLE GEOMETRY CONTROL SOLENOID VALVE CHECK
1. Disconnect the vacuum hoses from the variable geometry control solenoid valve.
noteWhen disconnecting the vacuum hose, always make sure that it can be reconnected at its original position.
2. Disconnect the harness connector.
3. Measure the resistance between the terminals of the variable geometry control solenoid valve.
Standard value: 10 to 14 Ω (at 20°C)
4. Replace the solenoid if airtightness or resistance is out of specification.

15 -INTAKE AND EXHAUST > TURBOCHARGER ASSEMBLY > TURBOCHARGER ASSEMBLY INSPECTION
INSPECTION
Check the following points; replace the part if a problem is found.
 
 
Turbocharger Assembly Check
1. Visually check the turbine wheel and the compressor wheel for cracking or other damage.
2. Check whether the turbine wheel and the compressor wheel can be easily turned by hand.
3. Check for oil leakage from the turbocharger assembly.
4. Check whether or not the turbocharger variable geometry actuator remains open. If any problem is found, replace the part after disassembly.
 
 
 
Oil Pipe and Oil Return Pipe Check
Check the oil pipe and oil return pipe for clogging, bending or other damage. If there is clogging, clean it.

Exhaust Manifold Check
1. Check for damage or cracking of any part.
2. Using a straight edge and a feeler gauge, check for distortion of the cylinder head or turbocharger side installation surface.

    • Standard value: 0.15 mm
    • Limit: 0.7 mm
  • <Turbocharger side>
    • Standard value: 0.1 mm
    • Limit: 0.5 mm
