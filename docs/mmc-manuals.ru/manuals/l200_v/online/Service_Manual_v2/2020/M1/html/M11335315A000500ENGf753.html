Code No. P2564: Variable Geometry Position Sensor Low <4N14>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • A power voltage of 5 V is applied to the variable geometry position sensor power terminal from the engine-ECU connector terminal VGP5 and earthed to the engine-ECU connector terminal VGPE from the variable geometry position sensor connector terminal.
  • The sensor signal is inputted to the engine-ECU connector terminal VGP from the variable geometry position sensor output terminal.
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The variable geometry position sensor detects the position of the variable geometry position and sends the signal to the engine-ECU.
  • Based on the signal, the engine-ECU performs the feedback control of the variable geometry position.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Battery positive voltage is 8 to 16 V.
  • 2 seconds later after the ignition switch has been in "ON" position or the engine has started up.
Judgement Criterion
  • The variable geometry position sensor output voltage is 0.20 V or less.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
None.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Failed turbocharger assembly (integrated with variable geometry position sensor).
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in variable geometry position sensor circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed engine-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data lists (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 559: Valve geometry position sensor
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Perform voltage measurement at variable geometry position sensor connector
  • Use special tool test harness(MB991658) to connect connector and measure at pick-up harness.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU VGP5 line and body earth.
OK: 4.9 to 5.1 V
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check harness between variable geometry position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal VGP5
Check power supply line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the connector(s), or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 4. Perform resistance measurement at variable geometry position sensor connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Resistance between terminal engine-ECU VGPE line and body earth.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 5.
 
STEP 5. Check harness between variable geometry position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal VGPE
Check earthing line for damage.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the connector(s), or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 6. Perform voltage measurement at variable geometry position sensor connector
  • Use special tool test harness (MB991658) to connect connector and measure at pick-up harness.
  • Engine: Idling (after warming up)
  • Voltage between terminal engine-ECU VGP line and body earth.
OK: 4.5 V or lower
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Go to Step 7.
 
STEP 7 Check harness between variable geometry position sensor connector terminal and engine-ECU connector terminal VGP
Check output line for open/short circuit and damage.
Is the check result normal?
Replace the turbocharger assembly (integrated with variable geometry position sensor).
Repair or replace the connector(s), or repair the damaged harness wire.
 
STEP 8. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position and start the engine.
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P2564 set?
Replace the engine-ECU.
