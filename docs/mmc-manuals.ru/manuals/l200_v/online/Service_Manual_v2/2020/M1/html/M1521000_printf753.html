52 -INTERIOR AND SUPPLEMENTAL RESTRAINT SYSTEM(SRS) > 52A-INTERIOR > INSIDE REAR VIEW MIRROR<AUTOMATIC ANTI-DAZZLING INSIDE REAR VIEW MIRROR> > SYMPTOM PROCEDURES > THE AUTOMATIC ANTI-GLARE INSIDE MIRROR CANNOT BE SET TO NIGHT MODE
Inspection Procedure 1: The automatic anti-dazzling mirror does not work normally.
 
 
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the ignition switch is turned to the "ON" position, the dimming function activates automatically. If the dimming function does not work normally, the inside rear view mirror, ignition switch circuit system, input circuit system of the back-up lamp switch <M/T>, input circuit system of the inhibitor switch <A/T> may be defective.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Damaged harness wires or connectors
  • Malfunction of the back-up lamp switch <M/T>
  • Malfunction of the inhibitor switch <A/T>
  • Malfunction of the inside rear view mirror
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Voltage measurement at inside rear view mirror connector (IG2 relay line).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness-side connector.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ACC" position.
(3) Voltage between inside rear view mirror connector (IG2 relay line) and the body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 2. Resistance measurement at inside rear view mirror connector (earth line).
(1) Disconnect inside rear view mirror connector, and measure the resistance available at the wiring harness side.
(2) Continuity between inside rear view mirror connector (earth line) and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (Less than 2 Ω)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 3. Voltage measurement at inside rear view mirror connector (IG1 relay line).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness-side connector.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3) Move the shift lever to the Reverse position. <M/T>
(4) Move the selector lever to the "R" position. <A/T>
(5) Voltage between inside rear view mirror connector (IG1 relay line) and the body earth
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
YES <M/T> : Go to Step 4.
YES <A/T> : Go to Step 5.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 4. Check the back-up lamp switch. <M/T>
Check that the back-up lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 22C - Transmission, Inspection .
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the back-lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 22C - Transmission ).
 
STEP 5. Check the inhibitor switch. <A/T>
Check that the inhibitor switch (Refer to GROUP 23C - On-vehicle Service, Inhibitor Switch Continuity Check ).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the inhibitor switch (Refer to GROUP 23D - Sensor ).
 
STEP 6. Check the inside rear view mirror.
Check that the harness connector is correctly mounted to the inside rear view mirror (Refer to ).
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction).
Replace the inside rear view mirror (Refer to ).
 
 
52 -INTERIOR AND SUPPLEMENTAL RESTRAINT SYSTEM(SRS) > 52A-INTERIOR > INSIDE REAR VIEW MIRROR<AUTOMATIC ANTI-DAZZLING INSIDE REAR VIEW MIRROR> > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > AUTO-DIMMING FEATURE TEST
AUTO-DIMMING FEATURE TEST
1. Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
2. Cover the forward-facing lamp sensor on the back of the mirror with a black cloth.
3. While in a well-lit area, make sure lamp strikes the sensor on the front of the mirror, simulating glare from vehicles behind you. The mirror will dim within 2 minutes if testing for the first time. If the mirror does not dim, replace the inside rear view mirror.
