GENERAL INFORMATION
Fuel tank is mounted on the front of the rear axle to reduce the shock at impact. A fuel shut-off valve, which prevents fuel leakage when the vehicle banks or rolls over, has been located inside the fuel tank for fewer openings and hose bonding points on the fuel tank top surface.

13 -FUEL > 13B-FUEL SUPPLY > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > FUEL TANK INSPECTION
FUEL TANK INSPECTION
noteDuring the check, the ambient temperature around the fuel tank should be as stable as possible (If the temperature changes, you can not measure the pressure correctly).
1.
cautionAlways check the fuel leak of fuel tank with the vehicle loaded. If it is checked with the fuel tank removed, the fuel tank may be damaged.
Remove the fuel tank cap, install special tool fuel leak check cap (MB992145).
2. Install the hose and pressure gauge to the special tool as shown.
3. Remove the rear body side skirt panel front (LH) <Club cab>.
4. Remove the rear wheelhouse splash shield (LH) (Refer to GROUP 42A - Splash Shield ) <Double cab>.
5. Block the fuel tank vapour hose to avoid damage.
6.
caution
  • Do not pressurise suddenly.
  • Do not pressurise 11.76 kPa or more to prevent the damage of components.
Pressurise while checking the pressure gauge and hold if it reaches from 9.80 to 11.76 kPa.
7. Leave it for approximately 3 minutes and check that the pressure is not weakened.
8. If the pressure is weakened, apply soapy water to each part with pressurised state and check the leak while confirming if air bubbles are seen.
 
13 -FUEL > 13B-FUEL SUPPLY > ON-VEHICLE SERVICE > FUEL DRAINING
FUEL DRAINING
caution
  • Working area should be airy and kept from flame.
  • Prepare a fire extinguisher before working.
  • Install the earth lines to the vehicle and fuel tank to prevent static electricity, and water the work place to keep the footing from slipping.
  • Touch the vehicle with hands to release the charged static.
  • Keep the drained fuel in the safety place.
1. Disconnect the fuel pipe on the side of fuel tank from the fuel hose on the front side of fuel tank.
2. Connect special tools fuel hand pump (MB992264), fuel hose adaptor A (MB992265) and 2 fuel hoses (Inner diameter 7.6 ± 0.4 mm) to the disconnected fuel tank hose.
3. Operate special tool (MB992264) to drain fuel from the fuel tank.

REMOVAL SERVICE POINT
 
 
<<A>> SELF-LOCKING NUT/FUEL TANK ASSEMBLY REMOVAL
1. Support the fuel tank assembly using the transmission jack, and remove self-locking nuts of the fuel tank assembly.
2. Using the transmission jack while taking care of the fuel harness, lower the fuel tank assembly until the fuel harness installation position over the fuel tank assembly can be checked.
3.
cautionBe careful not to open circuit in the harness when removing the fuel tank assembly.
Disconnect hoses, harness connector and harness clamp. Then, remove the fuel tank assembly.
 
 
 
 
INSTALLATION SERVICE POINT
 
 
>>A<< FUEL TANK ASSEMBLY/SELF-LOCKING NUT INSTALLATION
1. Place the fuel tank assembly on the transmission jack.
2. Raise the fuel tank to the position where the harness connector and harness clamp can be assembled.
3. Assemble the harness connector and harness clamp.
4. Use the transmission jack to install the fuel tank assembly to the vehicle.
5.
cautionWhen tightening the self-locking nut, be careful not to let the nut touch the bent part of the reinforcement of the front lower protector and the fuel tank.
Tighten a new self-locking nut to the specified torque and fix the fuel tank assembly.
Tightening torque: 55 ± 3 N·m

13 -FUEL > 13B-FUEL SUPPLY > FUEL FILTER > FUEL FILTER INSPECTION
INSPECTION
 
 
Water Level Switch Check
1. Connect the circuit tester to the water level switch.
2. There should be continuity when the float is raised, while there is no continuity when it is lowered.
3. Replace the fuel filter case if it is faulty.
 
 
 
Fuel Line Heater Check <4N14>
1. There should be continuity between the terminals when the fuel line heater body is cooled 0°C or bellow and continuity should disappear when the body is gradually heated.
If this is true then the heater is working properly.
2. Replace the fuel line heater if it is faulty.
