CHECK AT TRANSMISSION WIRING HARNESS CONNECTOR
1. Disconnect the A/T control solenoid valve assembly connector.
2. Using the special tool test harness (MB991709), measure the solenoid resistance.
Standard value:

3. If a solenoid fault is suspected, remove the valve body assembly to check.
 
 
 
CHECK AFTER VALVE BODY ASSEMBLY IS REMOVED
Remove the valve body assembly and inspect each solenoid installed on the valve body assembly by following the procedures below.

 
 
SL Solenoid Inspection
1.
caution
  • Do not remove the solenoid from the valve body assembly when measuring the resistance.
  • Do not damage the solenoid.
Use a multimeter to measure the resistance between the solenoid terminal and the solenoid body.
Standard value: 11 - 15 Ω (at 20°C)
2. Remove the solenoid.

3.
caution
  • Do not damage the solenoid.
  • Do not damage the solenoid terminal.
Connect the positive battery terminal to the solenoid terminal, and the negative terminal to the solenoid body, respectively. Then the solenoid should be activated.
4. When the solenoid is activated, air should flow through as shown in the figure.
5. Replace the valve body assembly if resistance or air flow is not normal.
 

 
 
Clutch Control Solenoid (S1, S2, S3, S4), Line Pressure Control Solenoid (SLT) and Lock-up Control Solenoid (SLU) Inspection
1.
caution
  • Do not remove the solenoid from the valve body assembly measuring the resistance.
  • Do not damage the solenoid.
Use a multimeter to measure the resistance between the solenoid terminals.
Standard value: 5.0 - 5.6 Ω (at 20°C)

2.
caution
  • Do not damage the solenoid.
  • Do not damage the solenoid terminal.
Connect a lamp bulb (12 V - 21 W) between the solenoid terminal No. 2 and the positive battery terminal.
3. Connect the solenoid terminal No. 1 to the negative battery terminal. Then the solenoid should be activated.
4. Replace the valve body assembly if the resistance is not normal or the solenoid is not activated.
 

 
 
Electro-magnetic Oil Pump (EMOP) <Vehicles with AS&G System>
1.
caution
  • Do not remove the electro-magnetic oil pump from the valve body assembly measuring the resistance.
  • Do not damage the electro-magnetic oil pump.
Use a multimeter to measure the resistance between the electro-magnetic oil pump terminals.
Standard value: 5.0 - 5.6 Ω (at 20°C)

2.
caution
  • Do not damage the electro-magnetic oil pump.
  • Do not damage the electro-magnetic oil pump terminal.
Connect a lamp bulb (12 V - 21 W) between the electro-magnetic oil pump terminal No. 1 and the positive battery terminal.
3. Connect the electro-magnetic oil pump terminal No. 2 to the negative battery terminal. Then the electro-magnetic oil pump should be activated.
4. Replace the valve body assembly if the resistance is not normal or the electro-magnetic oil pump is not activated.
