Inspection Procedure 3: Rear-view camera image is not correctly displayed. <Vehicles with MMCS and auto stop & go (AS&G) system>
 
 
cautionBefore replacing the rear-view camera or multivision display, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit, and the communication circuit are normal (Check that the voltage is 10 V or more).
 
 
COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the screen of rear-view camera is not shown even if the shift lever is "R" (Reverse) position, the rear-view camera, multivision display, the wiring harness, connectors, back-up lamp switch may be defective.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the rear-view camera
  • Malfunction of the multivision display
  • Malfunction of the DC/DC converter
  • Malfunction of the back-up lamp switch
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the multivision display operation.
Check if the multivision display normally and the sound is output.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Troubleshoot the MMCS (Refer to ).
 
STEP 2. Confirmation the service mode.
  1. Display the "Vehicle Signal Check" screen (Refer to ).
  2. Move the shift lever to the "R" (reverse) position.
  3. Check that "Shift Position R" is "ON."
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check the back-up lamp switch.
Check the continuity of the back-up lamp switch. (Refer to GROUP 22C - Transmission )
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Replace the back-up lamp switch.
 
STEP 4. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in REV line between multivision display connector and back-up lamp switch connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 5. Confirmation the service mode.
  1. Display the "Communications & Connection Check" screen (Refer to ).
  2. Check that "Rear Camera" is "OK."
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Go to Step 6.
 
STEP 6. Check the DC/DC converter.
Check the continuity of the DC/DC converter.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Replace the DC/DC converter.
 
STEP 7. Voltage measurement at DC/DC converter connector (IGN terminal).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to the "ACC" position.
(3) Check the voltage between DC/DC converter connector (IGN terminal) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
NO <vehicles without KOS> : Go to Step 8.
NO <vehicles with KOS> : Go to Step 9.
 
STEP 8. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in ACC line between ignition switch (ACC) and DC/DC converter connector.
Is the check result normal?
Refer to Inspection Procedure "Malfunction of the ignition switch power supply system" .
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 9. Check the M.U.T.-III Diagnosis code for other systems.
Check that the KOS&OSS-ECU sets a diagnosis code.
Is the diagnosis code set?
Diagnose the KOS&OSS-ECU. Refer to GROUP 42B, Troubleshooting .
Go to Step 10.
 
STEP 10. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in IGN line between ACC relay and DC/DC converter connector.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 11. Voltage measurement at DC/DC converter connector (PWR terminal).
(1) Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Check the voltage between DC/DC converter connector (PWR terminal) and body earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Go to Step 12.
 
STEP 12. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in PWR line between fusible link and DC/DC converter connector.
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction is suspected (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 13. Check the wiring harness between the DC/DC converter connector (OUT1 terminal) and the multivision display connector (+B2 terminal).
  • Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit between DC/DC converter connector (OUT1 terminal) and multivision display connector (+B2 terminal).
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair or replace the connector, or check and the repair wiring harness between fusible link and multivision display connector terminal +B2.
 
STEP 14. Check of short to earth, and open circuit in GND2 line between multivision display connector and body earth.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 15. Check of short to power supply, short to earth, and open circuit in CACC, CGND, CDET, CMP- and CMP+ line between multivision display connector and rear-view camera connector.
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Repair the wiring harness.
 
STEP 16. Trouble symptom recheck.
Check that the display of rear-view camera is shown normally.
Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Replace the rear-view camera temporarily and go to Step 17.
 
STEP 17. Trouble symptom recheck.
Check that the display of rear-view camera is shown normally.
Is the check result normal?
Replace the rear-view camera.
Replace the multivision connector.
