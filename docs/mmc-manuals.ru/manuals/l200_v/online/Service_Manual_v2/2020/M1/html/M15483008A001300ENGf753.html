Diagnosis Item 3: Diagnose shorts in the earth to CAN bus line (CAN-C). <RHD>
 
 
cautionWhen servicing a CAN bus line, earth yourself by touching a metal object such as an unpainted water pipe. If you fail to do, a component connected to the CAN bus line may be broken.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT CONDITIONS
The M.U.T.-III judges the trouble when it is impossible to receive the periodically sent data and the voltage of CAN_H or CAN_L line is less than 1.0 V.
 
 
TROUBLESHOOTING HINTS
  • Malfunction of the wiring harness
  • Malfunction of the connector
  • Malfunction of the ETACS-ECU
  • Malfunction of the combination meter
  • Malfunction of the transfer-ECU <4WD>
  • Malfunction of the KOS&OSS-ECU <Vehicles with KOS>
  • Malfunction of the SRS-ECU
  • Malfunction of the ASTC-ECU
  • Malfunction of the A/T-ECU <A/T>
  • Malfunction of the AS&G-ECU <Vehicles with AS&G system>
  • Malfunction of the FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU <Vehicles with FCM/LDW/AHB-ECU>
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU
  • Malfunction of the steering wheel sensor (column switch assembly)
  • Malfunction of the clearance warning control unit <Vehicles with corner sensor system>
  • Malfunction of the gateway-ECU (for BSW) <Vehicles with Blind Spot Warning [BSW] (with Lane Change Assist) system>
  • Malfunction of the gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C) <Vehicles with gateway-ECU (for GW-CAN-C)>
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. Check the CAN bus lines. Resistance measurement at diagnosis connector.
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal.
noteThis inspection allows you to check that there is a short to earth in either CAN_H line or CAN_L line. Thus, in the following steps, check the CAN bus line that is defective.
(1) Disconnect engine-ECU connector and ETACS-ECU connector, and measure the resistance at harness side of diagnosis connector.
(2) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(3) Resistance between diagnosis connector terminal No. 6 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 1 kΩ or more
(4) Resistance between diagnosis connector terminal No. 14 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 1 kΩ or more
Is the check result normal?
<Both of the measurement results show 1 kΩ or more> Go to Step 42.
<Either of CAN_H line or CAN_L line the measurement results show less than 1 kΩ> Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Check the CAN line. Resistance measurement at joint connector (CAN2).
caution
  • A digital multimeter should be used.
  • The test wiring harness should be used.
  • When measuring the resistance, disconnect the negative battery terminal.
(1) Disconnect joint connector (CAN2), and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2) Ensure that the negative battery terminal is disconnected.
(3) Resistance between joint connector (CAN2) terminal No. 10 (CAN_H) and body earth
OK: 1 kΩ or more
(4) Resistance between joint connector (CAN2) terminal No. 21 (CAN_L) and body earth
OK: 1 kΩ or more
Is the check result normal?
<1 kΩ or more> Go to Step 19.
<Less than 1 kΩ > Go to Step 3.
