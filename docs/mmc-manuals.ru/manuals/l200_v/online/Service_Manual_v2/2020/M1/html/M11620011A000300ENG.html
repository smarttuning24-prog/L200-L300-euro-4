Pinion Gap Adjustment
1. Disconnect the field coil wire from the M-terminal of the magnetic switch.
2. Connect a 12-volt battery between the S-terminal and M-terminal.
3.
cautionThis test must be performed quickly (in less than 10 seconds) to prevent the coil from burning.
Set the switch to ON, and the pinion will move out.
4. Check the pinion-to-stopper clearance (pinion gap) with a feeler gauge.
Standard value: 0.5 to 2.0 mm
5. If the pinion gap is out of specification, replace the starter assembly.
 
 
 
Magnetic Switch Pull-in Test
1. Disconnect the field coil wire from the M-terminal of the magnetic switch.
2.
cautionThis test must be performed quickly (in less than 10 seconds) to prevent the coil from burning.
Connect a 12-volt battery between the S-terminal and M-terminal.
3. If the pinion moves out, the pull-in coil is good. If it doesn't, replace the starter assembly.
 
 
 
Magnetic Switch Hold-in Test
1. Disconnect the field coil wire from the M-terminal of the magnetic switch.
2.
cautionThis test must be performed quickly (in less than 10 seconds) to prevent the coil from burning.
Connect a 12-volt battery between the S-terminal and body.
3. Manually pull out the pinion as far as the pinion stopper position.
4. If the pinion remains out, everything is in order. If the pinion moves in, the hold-in circuit is open. Replace the starter assembly.
 
 
 
Free Running Test
1. Place the starter assembly in a vise equipped with soft jaws and connect a fully-charged 12-volt battery to the starter assembly as follows:
2. Connect a test ammeter (150-ampere scale) and carbon pile rheostat in series between the battery (+) terminal and starter assembly terminal.
3. Connect a voltmeter (15-volt scale) across the starter assembly.
4. Rotate the rheostat to full-resistance position.
5. Connect the battery cable from the battery (-) terminal to the starter assembly body.
6. Adjust the rheostat until the battery voltage shown on the voltmeter is 11 V.
7. Confirm that the maximum amperage is within 130 A and that the starter assembly turns smoothly and freely.
 
 
 
Magnetic Switch Return Test
1. Disconnect the field coil wire from the M-terminal of the magnetic switch.
2.
cautionThis test must be performed quickly (in less than 10 seconds) to prevent the coil from burning.
Connect a 12-volt battery between the M-terminal and body.
3.
warningBe careful not to get your fingers caught when pulling out the pinion.
Pull the pinion out and release. If the pinion quickly returns to its original position, everything is operating properly. If it doesn't, replace the starter assembly.
