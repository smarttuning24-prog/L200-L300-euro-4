Code No. P081D: Neutral Position Switch Circuit <M/T>
 
 
 
 
OPERATION
  • The power voltage is supplied to the neutral position switch connector terminal from the J/B connector terminal.
  • The ON/OFF signal is inputted to the AS&G-ECU connector terminal NTSW from the neutral position switch connector terminal.
 
 
FUNCTION
  • The AS&G-ECU detects the gearshift lever position by using the signals of the neutral position switch.
  • In response to the signal, the AS&G-ECU controls the AS&G system.
 
 
TROUBLE JUDGEMENT
Check Conditions
  • Ignition switch is "ON" position.
  • The diagnosis code No. P0833 is not detected.
  • Ambient temperature is more than -5°C.
  • The diagnosis code No. U0141 is not detected.
  • The diagnosis code No. U0100 is not detected.
Judgement Criterion
  • When you drive the vehicle at more than 60 km/h at least one time, the ON/OFF signal of the neutral position switch does not change even once although the ON/OFF signal of the clutch interlock switch is detected more than 10 times.
 
 
FAIL-SAFE AND BACKUP FUNCTION
In the normal operation, the auto stop is prohibited.
 
 
PROBABLE CAUSES
  • Open/short circuit or harness damage in neutral position switch circuit or loose connector contact.
  • Failed neutral position switch.
  • Failed installation of neutral position switch.
  • Failed system inside transmission.
  • Failed AS&G-ECU.
 
 
DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE
STEP 1. M.U.T.-III data list
cautionTo prevent damage to the M.U.T.-III, always turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position before connecting or disconnecting the M.U.T.-III.
Use M.U.T.-III to check the data lists (Refer to ).
  • Item No. 8: Neutral Position SW
OK:
  • ON (Gearshift lever position: Neutral)
  • OFF (Gearshift lever position: Except neutral)
Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
Go to Step 2.
 
STEP 2. Continuity check on neutral position switch connector
  • Disconnect connector, and check at neutral position switch side.
  • Continuity check between the neutral position switch terminals.
OK:
  • Continuity (Gearshift lever position: Neutral)
  • No continuity (Gearshift lever position: Except neutral)
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Go to Step 3.
 
STEP 3. Check the installation state of the neutral position switch
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair.
 
STEP 4. Check the neutral position switch itself
Check the neutral position switch itself (Refer to GROUP 22B - Transmission, Transmission Inspection, Neutral Position Switch Check <R6M5A>, or Refer to GROUP 22C - Transmission, Transmission Inspection, Neutral Position Switch Check <V6M5A>).
Is the check result normal?
The area affecting the neutral position switch inside the transmission can possibly be abnormal. Overhaul and repair the transmission, or change the transmission.
Replace the neutral position switch.
 
STEP 5. Perform voltage measurement at neutral position switch connector
  • Disconnect connector, and measure at harness side.
  • Ignition switch: "ON" position
  • Voltage between terminal IG1 line and earth.
OK: System voltage
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair the connector(s), or check short to earth, and open circuit in IG1 line between J/B connector and neutral position switch connector.
 
STEP 6. Check of harness damage in IG1 line between J/B connector and neutral position switch connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 7. Check of short to earth, open circuit and harness damage in NTSW line between neutral position switch connector and AS&G-ECU connector
Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the connector(s) or wiring harness.
 
STEP 8. Diagnosis code recheck after erasing diagnosis code
Check again if the diagnosis code is set.
(1) Use M.U.T.-III to erase the diagnosis code.
(2) Turn the ignition switch to "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3) Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(4) Use M.U.T.-III to recheck the diagnosis code.
Is diagnosis code No. P081D set?
Replace the AS&G-ECU.
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions ).
